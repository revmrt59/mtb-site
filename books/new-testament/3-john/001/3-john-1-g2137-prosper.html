Summary:

PROSPER - (euodoo, Strong’s G2137): To experience favorable progress in life’s affairs under God’s ordering and blessing.

Full Study:

PROSPER - (euodoo, Strong’s G2137)

  1. Greek roots / derivation
    Eu (good, well) + hodos (way, road). Literally, to have a good journey or good road.

  2. Meaning / definition
    To prosper, to succeed, to have favorable progress in one’s affairs.

  3. Usage in Greek culture in Biblical times
    Used for travel success and by extension successful outcomes in business or plans.

  4. Theological implications
    Scripture recognizes that God can grant favorable outcomes, but it does not reduce blessing to money or comfort.

  5. Contextual nuance in the verse
    John prays that Gaius would “prosper in all things,” meaning his practical life would go well, consistent with his spiritual stability.

  6. Key uses in other verses (full NKJV)
    Romans 1:10
    Making request if, by some means, now at last I may find a way in the will of God to come to you. (NKJV)
    Note: the idea of being enabled to have a successful journey aligns with euodoo even where the exact English term varies.

1 Corinthians 16:2
On the first day of the week let each one of you lay something aside, storing up as he may prosper (euodootai), that there be no collections when I come. (NKJV)

  1. Other insights
    John sets prosperity under the authority of soul health. Inner order first, outer order second.
