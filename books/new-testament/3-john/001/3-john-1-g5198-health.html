HEALTH - (hugiano, Strong’s G5198)

Wholeness and soundness, referring both to physical well-being and overall condition.

Full Study:

HEALTH - (hugiano, Strong’s G5198)

  1. Greek roots / derivation
    From a root meaning sound, whole. Source of English words related to hygiene.

  2. Meaning / definition
    To be healthy, to be sound. Can refer to physical health or soundness in teaching.

  3. Usage in Greek culture in Biblical times
    Used for bodily well-being and for the idea of something being “sound” or intact.

  4. Theological implications
    God cares about wholeness. Scripture can speak of bodily healing and also “sound doctrine,” both tied to the idea of what is healthy.

  5. Contextual nuance in the verse
    John prays for physical well-being for Gaius. It is pastoral, not materialistic.

  6. Key uses in other verses (full NKJV)
    Luke 5:31
    Jesus answered and said to them, “Those who are well (hugiainontes) have no need of a physician, but those who are sick.” (NKJV)

Titus 2:1
But as for you, speak the things which are proper for sound (hugiousa) doctrine. (NKJV)

  1. Other insights
    This word ties bodily health and doctrinal health to the same basic idea: soundness.
