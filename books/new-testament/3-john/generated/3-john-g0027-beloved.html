Summary:

BELOVED - (agapetos, Strong’s G0027): One who is deeply cherished in covenant love, reflecting familial affection within the body of Christ.

Full Study:

BELOVED - (agapetos, Strong’s G27)

  1. Greek roots / derivation
    From agapao, to love with covenant, self-giving love.

  2. Meaning / definition
    Dearly loved, cherished. Often used as a title of affection and honor.

  3. Usage in Greek culture in Biblical times (with examples if relevant)
    Used for close personal relationships. In Christian use it becomes a marker of family-level affection among believers.

  4. Theological implications
    Christian instruction is meant to be delivered inside love. The gospel creates a family, not merely an organization.

  5. Contextual nuance in the verse
    John repeatedly calls Gaius “beloved” because his goal is encouragement and strengthening, even when problems are addressed later.

  6. Key uses in other verses (full NKJV)
    Matthew 3:17
    And suddenly a voice came from heaven, saying, “This is My beloved (agapetos) Son, in whom I am well pleased.” (NKJV)

Romans 12:19
Beloved (agapetoi), do not avenge yourselves, but rather give place to wrath. (NKJV)

  1. Other insights
    “Beloved” is not sentimental filler. It frames the letter as shepherding.
