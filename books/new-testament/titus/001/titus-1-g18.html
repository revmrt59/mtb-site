Summary

GOOD — (agathos, Strong—s G18)
Refers to that which is intrinsically beneficial, upright, and honorable. In the closing of Titus 1, it defines the standard for "works" that are divinely approved—actions that flow from a pure heart and serve a noble, God-honoring purpose.

Full Study:

1. Greek Roots / Derivation
A primary word (agathos) describing that which is "good" in its nature and "beneficial" in its effect. It is often contrasted with kakos (evil/harmful). Bible Hub

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition
Excellence of character; useful, salutary, or honorable. It describes something that is not only "good" in a moral sense but also "good" in a functional sense—it accomplishes something beneficial for others. Thayer—s Greek Lexicon

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Fertile Soil: In Greek agriculture, agathos was used for land that produced a rich harvest. It wasn't just "dirt"; it was "good" because it was productive and life-giving.

  • The Honorable Citizen: In civic life, it described a man of noble character who contributed to the welfare of the city. He was "good" because his life added value to those around him. Precept Austin

  • The Healthy Tree: A common word picture is that of a sound tree that inevitably bears "good" fruit. The fruit is "good" because the root is healthy.

4. Theological Implications
In the New Testament, "good" is not a human achievement but a reflection of God's nature. Jesus said, "No one is good but One, that is, God" (Matthew 19:17). Therefore, "good works" are only possible when a person is rightly related to the source of all goodness. They are the "fruit of the Spirit," proving that the believer has been "created in Christ Jesus for good (agathos) works" (Ephesians 2:10).
