Titus 1:6 Husband of one wife (mias gynaikos anôr) faithful to his wife A one-woman man; sexually faithful; covenant loyal Describes covenant loyalty and marital faithfulness. Leadership begins with faithfulness in the most intimate and revealing relationship.

Or Children marked by faith and submission within the household Emphasizes a home shaped by faith rather than perfection. The focus is not flawless outcomes but consistent discipleship, loving authority, and credible spiritual influence. The home is a proving ground for leadership.

Titus 1:6 Dissipation (asìtia) Being wild Reckless, wasteful, morally abandoned living Describes a lifestyle pattern, not isolated failure. This guards against a home slowly shaped by indulgence and undisciplined living rather than ongoing discipleship.
Titus 1:6 Insubordination (anupotakta) Rebellious Refusal to submit to authority Links household order with church order. A man who cannot shepherd submission in his own home will struggle to shepherd hearts in the church.
Titus 1:7 Bishop (episkopos) Overseer One who watches over; guardian Describes function, not title; Elder (presbuteros) and Overseer (episkopos) are used interchangeably to describe those who govern and lead a local church. Elder speaks to the character of the office holder while Overseer speaks to the duties. Elders oversee and manage God—s household as entrusted stewards, not owners. Authority in the church is always exercised under God, never independent of Him, and always for the good of others.
Titus 1:7 Blameless (anegklôtos) Blameless life Not open to legitimate charge Repetition stresses priority and seriousness. Character is not a secondary concern but the foundation of trust.
Titus 1:7 Steward (oikonomos) Manager of God's household One entrusted with another—s household Leaders manage God—s church according to His will and direction. Stewards do not ask, “What do I want?” but “What does the Owner desire for His house?”
Titus 1:7 Self-willed (authadôs) Not arrogant Self-pleasing; insists on own way Directly contradicts the idea of stewardship. This trait fractures community by elevating personal preference above God—s purposes and people—s care.
Titus 1:7 Quick-tempered (orgilos) Not quick tempered Prone to anger; easily provoked Anger that flares quickly cannot patiently carry wounded or resistant people toward maturity. It can also indicate unhealed areas in a leader.
Titus 1:7 Given to wine (paroinos) Not a heavy drinker Habitually associated with alcohol Refers to pattern of indulgence, not mere use. Leaders must model freedom from mastery and escape-driving liviing, showing that joy and rest are found in God.
Titus 1:7 Violent (plôktôs) Violent Physically aggressive; a striker Shepherds must lead gently, not forcefully or aggressively. Leaders lead through truth and love, not pressure, intimidation, or force.
Titus 1:7 Greedy for money (aischrokerdôs) Dishonest with money Seeking gain through improper or shameful means Prepares for warnings about false teachers. When gain becomes the motive, people eventually become means instead of souls.
