Summary

DOCTRINE - (didaskalia, Strong’s G1319)

Summary: Refers to the act of teaching or the content of the instruction itself. It is the body of truth that reveals God's heart and provides the foundation for a life that is "proper" for the believer.

Full Study:

  1. Greek Roots / Derivation: From didaskalos (a teacher) and the root da- (to learn). It describes the systematic communication of truth.

  2. Meaning / Lexical Definition: The act of teaching, instruction, or the body of information taught. In the New Testament, it specifically refers to the authoritative teaching of the Gospel.

  3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures:

    • The Master Apprentice: In ancient culture, didaskalia was the process by which a master passed on the essential skills of a trade to an apprentice.

    • The Philosophical School: It described the core tenets and beliefs that defined a specific school of thought.

    • The Mental Image: Think of a roadmap provided by a guide; the didaskalia is the reliable set of directions that ensures the traveler stays on the right path.

  4. Theological Implications: Doctrine is the foundation for transformation. It is not merely information to be known, but life-giving revelation that produces godliness. True didaskalia always points back to Jesus.

  5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:1: The NKJV says: "But as for you, speak the things which are proper for sound doctrine (didaskalia)". Paul is emphasizing that the content of Titus’s teaching must be healthy and functional.

  6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV):

    • 1 Timothy 4:13: "Till I come, give attention to reading, to exhortation, to doctrine (didaskalia)."

    • 1 Timothy 4:16: "Take heed to yourself and to the doctrine (didaskalia). Continue in them..."

    • 2 Timothy 3:16: "All Scripture is given by inspiration of God, and is profitable for doctrine (didaskalia)..."

  7. Other Relevant Insights: Doctrine serves as a guardrail. It protects the church from "myths" and ensures teaching remains centered on the finished work of Christ.
