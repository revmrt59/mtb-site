Summary

SOUND - (hugiainō, Strong’s G1519)

Summary: Refers to that which is healthy, wholesome, and free from disease or defect. In a teaching context, it describes doctrine that is "uncorrupted" and capable of producing spiritual health and vitality in the hearer.

Full Study:

  1. Greek Roots / Derivation: From hugiēs (well, whole, healthy). It is the root of our English word "hygiene".

  2. Meaning / Lexical Definition: To be in good health; to be healthy. In the pastoral epistles, it is used metaphorically to describe teaching that is "true to the original" and free from the "sickness" of false teaching.

  3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures:

    • The Physician’s Report: In secular Greek, it was a medical term used to describe a body that was functioning exactly as it was designed, with no infection or injury.

    • The Solid Foundation: It was used for a building that was "sound"-meaning it had no hidden cracks or structural weaknesses.

    • The Mental Image: Think of a pure mountain spring versus a stagnant pond; one is "sound" and brings life, while the other is "unhealthy" and brings disease.

  4. Theological Implications: Doctrine is not just a set of correct facts; it is "spiritual medicine". If doctrine is "sound," it inevitably produces health in the person who believes it. The nature of the message determines the health of the church.

  5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:1: The NKJV says: "But as for you, speak the things which are proper for sound (hugiainō) doctrine". This suggests the health of the church depended on Titus sticking to the "wholesome" Gospel that produces actual godliness.

  6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV):

    • 1 Timothy 1:10: "...and if there is any other thing that is contrary to sound (hugiainō) doctrine,"

    • 2 Timothy 1:13: "Hold fast the form of sound (hugiainō) words which you have heard from me,"

    • Luke 5:31: "Jesus answered and said to them, 'Those who are well (hugiainō) have no need of a physician, but those who are sick.'"

  7. Other Relevant Insights: The emphasis here is on reproducibility. "Sound" doctrine isn't just "right"-it is "functional". It works as designed to form the image of Christ in a person.
