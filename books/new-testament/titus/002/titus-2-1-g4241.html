Summary

PROPER — (prepō, Strong’s G4241)

Summary:: Refers to that which is conspicuously fit, suitable, or becoming for a specific person or position. It describes conduct that matches the nature of the Gospel, ensuring the believer’s life does not contradict the truth they profess.

Full Study:

  1. Greek Roots / Derivation: Derived from a primary verb meaning to be eminent or to stand out. It carries the idea of being clearly seen as "right" for the occasion.

  2. Meaning / Lexical Definition: To be seemly, fit, or suitable. In the New Testament, it is used to describe behavior that is appropriate for those who belong to God.

  3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures:

    • The Perfectly Tailored Garment: In secular Greek, prepō described a garment that was so perfectly suited to the wearer that it looked exactly right on them.

    • The Royal Entrance: It was used for the dignity and decorum expected in a king’s presence.

    • The Mental Image: Think of a master builder using the "proper" tool for a specific task; the tool is chosen because it is the only one that can do the job correctly.

  4. Theological Implications: Holiness is not just avoiding sin; it is doing what is "proper". Our conduct acts as a frame for the Gospel; when we live in a way that is "becoming" to our call, we make the truth of God attractive and clear to those watching.

  5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:1: The NKJV says: "But as for you, speak the things which are proper (prepō) for sound doctrine". Paul is telling Titus that "sound doctrine" must result in a certain kind of living. If the way we live does not match the Word we teach, it is "unbecoming" and brings confusion.

  6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV):

    • Matthew 3:15: "But Jesus answered and said to him, 'Permit it to be so now, for thus it is fitting (prepō) for us to fulfill all righteousness.'"

    • Ephesians 5:3: "But fornication and all uncleanness or covetousness, let it not even be named among you, as is fitting (prepō) for saints;"

    • Hebrews 2:10: "For it was fitting (prepō) for Him, for whom are all things and by whom are all things, in bringing many sons to glory..."

  7. Other Relevant Insights: In the ancient world, doing what was prepō meant living according to one's true identity. For the believer, it means our outward life finally lines up with who we have become in Christ.
