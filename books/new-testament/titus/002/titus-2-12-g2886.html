Summary

WORLDLY - (kosmikos, Strong’s G2886)

Summary: Refers to that which belongs to the world's system or the current age. It describes a focus or quality that is "proper" to this earthly sphere but "unhealthy" and out of place for a citizen of heaven.

Full Study:

  1. Greek Roots / Derivation: Derived from kosmos (order, system, or world). In the New Testament, kosmos often refers to the world system that is currently alienated from God.

  2. Meaning / Lexical Definition: Of or belonging to the world; secular; earthly. It pertains to the values, perspectives, and "intoxicants" of the present age.

  3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures:

    • The Foreign Garment: In secular Greek, it was used to describe something that fit the customs of the "world" at large rather than a specific local culture.

    • The Temporary Structure: It pictured a building or ornament that was made for the present moment but lacked "eternal" durability.

    • The Mental Image: Think of a stage set for a play; it looks real and has "proper" order within the theater, but it is kosmikos-it is only for the "show" and is not the "sound" reality of the world outside.

  4. Theological Implications: To be "worldly" is to have an "internal hygiene" problem. It means the soul is being nourished by the "diseased" values of a system that is passing away. Since God is "infinitely good" and eternal, anything that is purely kosmikos is "unfit" for the "Highway of Holiness." It represents the "leprous" tendency of the heart to settle for the "shadow" rather than the substance.

  5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:12: The NKJV says: “...denying ungodliness and worldly (kosmikos) lusts...”. Paul uses this to qualify the "lusts" we are to deny. It isn't just any desire; it is the specific "reaching out" after the world's "unhealthy" definition of success, pleasure, and identity.

  6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV):

    • Hebrews 9:1: “Then indeed, even the first covenant had... the earthly (kosmikos) sanctuary.” (Distinguishing the physical tabernacle from the "sound," heavenly one).

  7. Other Relevant Insights: This word identifies the "source" of our distractions. When we are kosmikos, we lack "meaning integrity" because our lives don't match our heavenly citizenship. Denying the "worldly" is the "action" of protecting our "spiritual container" from the "pollution" of the current age.

  8. References: Blue Letter Bible: Strong's G2886 - kosmikos; Thayer’s Greek Lexicon; Vine’s Expository Dictionary: Worldly.
