Summary

TEACHING - (paideuō, Strong’s G3811) Refers to the process of training, disciplining, and educating a child. It describes the "active" work of grace as a tutor that not only provides information but also corrects and shapes the believer into a "sound" and mature state.

Full Study:

1. Greek Roots / Derivation Derived from pais, meaning "a child." It is the root from which we get "pedagogy."

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition To train children; to instruct, to discipline, or to correct. It carries the idea of "upbringing" and "nurture." In the New Testament, it often refers to the firm but loving "pruning" and "training" that leads a person toward "sacred fitness."

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures * The Spartan Training: In secular Greek, paideuō was used for the rigorous training of young men to become soldiers. It involved learning what to "embrace" (courage) and what to "deny" (cowardice).

  • The Sculptor's Chisel: It pictured the way a sculptor removes the "unhealthy" and excess stone to reveal the intended "pattern" within.

  • The Mental Image: Think of a parent teaching a child to walk; they provide both the "support" to stand and the "correction" when the child veers toward danger. "Teaching" is the "internal hygiene" that removes the "rot" of the old life.

4. Theological Implications Grace is not just a "rescue" from the past; it is a "tutor" for the present. The grace that "appeared" (epiphainō) now stays to "teach" us. It is the "functional" side of salvation-ensuring the believer is "wholly His" by replacing "unhealthy" desires with "sound" character. It proves that the "Highway of Holiness" is a path of constant growth and "proper" discipline.

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:12 The NKJV says: “teaching (paideuō) us that, denying ungodliness and worldly lusts...”. Paul presents grace as a schoolmaster. It doesn't just "overlook" sin; it "trains" us out of it. This "sacred fitness" is the direct result of being in the "classroom of grace," where we learn to live in a way that is "becoming" to the Gospel.

6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV) * Acts 7:22: “And Moses was learned (paideuō) in all the wisdom of the Egyptians...” * 1 Corinthians 11:32: “But when we are judged, we are chastened (paideuō) by the Lord, that we may not be condemned with the world.” * Hebrews 12:6: “For whom the Lord loves He chastens (paideuō)...”

7. Other Relevant Insights This word represents the "reproducible" discipline of the Christian life. To be under the paideuō of grace is to have a "safe mind" that recognizes the "meaning integrity" of God's rules. It is the "action" of grace that ensures our "spiritual container" is constantly being cleaned and prepared for "good works."
