Summary

ZEALOUS — (zēlōtēs, Strong’s G2207)

  • Refers to one who is deeply committed, burning with enthusiasm, or intensely devoted to a cause .

  • Zeal is the inward fire that fuels outward "good works," ensuring that the truth is "walked out" rather than merely discussed .

Full Study

Greek Roots / Derivation

Derived from zeō, meaning "to boil" or "to be hot". It describes a person whose heart is at a "boil" for the things of God, directly opposing the "cynicism" or "withdrawal" that can plague those who are not "sound in faith".

Meaning / Lexical Definition

An enthusiast, a partisan, or a loyal adherent. In Titus 2, it describes a people who are not just doing their duty, but are "zealously preparing" themselves for the return of the Bridegroom .

Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Burning Ember: Used to describe a coal that is glowing red-hot, capable of igniting everything it touches.

  • The Competitive Athlete: It carried the mental image of an athlete who exerts every ounce of energy to win a prize.

  • The Mental Image: Think of a heart that is no longer "cold" or "hardened" by years in the kingdom, but is kept at a boil by the "blessed hope" .

Theological Implications

To be zealous (G2207) means our capacity to serve is healthy and free from the "sickness" of apathy. This zeal closes the "integrity gap" because it replaces religious obligation with a volitional choice of the will to follow Jesus.

Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:14

The NKJV says: "...and purify for Himself His own special people, zealous (zēlōtēs) for good works."

  • Paul shows that the result of being "purified" is a life that is "walked out" with passion and excellence .

  • This zeal is the "manifestation" of a life that ascribes heaven's value to every daily task, whether in the home or the workplace.

Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • Acts 21:20: "...and they are all zealous (zēlōtēs) for the law."

  • 1 Corinthians 14:12: "...since you are zealous (zēlōtēs) for spiritual gifts, seek to excel..."

  • Galatians 1:14: "...being more exceedingly zealous (zēlōtēs) for the traditions of my fathers."

Other Relevant Insights

  • Biblical Context: In the Old Testament, Phinehas was considered "zealous" for God’s honor, a zeal that turned away wrath and established a covenant of peace.

  • Textual Application: This zeal prevents "coasting into eternity" and instead drives us to be a people found ready when our Beloved returns .

  • Biblical Culture: The term was also associated with the "Zealots," a political-religious group in Israel whose intense devotion to God’s sovereignty made them willing to sacrifice everything for their cause.
