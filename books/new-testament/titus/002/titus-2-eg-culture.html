East Gate Culture in Titus 2

In Titus 2, Paul provides a clear framework for how the gospel creates a healthy, functional community. This chapter illustrates that when we live out our identity as sons and daughters, we become the place where heaven touches earth.

HEALTH | From the Top Down and Inside Out

  • The Power of Sound Doctrine: We believe that healthy doctrine is the spiritual medicine that produces healthy lives.

  • Trickle-Down Health: Spiritual health spreads from the top down; if the older men and leaders are steady and "sound in faith," the entire church remains steady.

  • Spiritual and Emotional Care: We are healthy when our thoughts, feelings, and desires are trained by grace to reflect God's heart.

EXCELLENCE | Your Best, His Glory

  • Unseen Integrity: We choose to do our best even when no one is watching, mirroring the "good fidelity" required of workers in the workplace.

  • Adorning the Word: We strive for excellence in our daily work to "adorn the doctrine," making the truth of God attractive to everyone around us.

  • His Worthiness: Our commitment to excellence is rooted in the fact that He is always worthy of our highest standard.

COMMUNICATION | Real Talk for Real Relationships

  • Verbal Clarity: Following Paul—s mandate to "speak," we choose to have 100% of our conversations with honesty and open hearts.

  • Authenticity over Comfort: We "real talk" about the things people need to hear, even when it is awkward or causes opposition, because it leads to stronger relationships tomorrow.

  • Trust and Truth: Our relationships are built on the trust that comes from speaking things that are "proper for sound doctrine".

PEOPLE OF THE WORD | Foundations for Life

  • Truth Walked Out: We believe that the Holy Scriptures are not just for reading but are meant to be walked out as a community.

  • The Inerrant Foundation: We hold fast to the timeless Word of God as the only foundation that can correct disorder and bring people to maturity.

  • Corporate Growth: As a people of the Word, we discuss and apply the Scriptures corporately to ensure every age group is "sound" and "stable".

AWAITING THE RETURN | Our Blessed Hope

  • The Great Motivation: Our desire for godliness is fueled by the "blessed hope"—the certain and glorious return of Jesus Christ.

  • Ready for the Beloved: We live as a "special people," zealously preparing ourselves so that we are found ready when our Beloved returns.

  • Expectant Preparation: It is our honor to live soberly and godly in this present age as we wait expectantly for Him to return for His bride.
