Summary

RIGHTEOUSLY — (dikaiìs, Strong—s G1346) Refers to living in a way that is just, upright, and in accordance with God—s "sound" law. It describes the external application of a "safe mind," where the believer—s actions toward others are "proper," fair, and reflect the "infinitely good" character of God.

Full Study:

1. Greek Roots / Derivation The adverbial form of dikaios (just, righteous), which comes from dikô (a judicial verdict or right). It describes the manner in which a person conducts their affairs.

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition Justly, uprightly, or in a right manner. It denotes a life lived in "proper" alignment with what is right in the sight of God and man. It is the "functional" evidence of a heart that has been made "sound" by grace.

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Straight Path: In secular Greek, dikaiìs was used to describe a path that was "straight" and "level." It didn't "deviate" into the "unhealthy" ditches of dishonesty or partiality.

  • The Honest Merchant: It pictured a businessman who used "accurate" weights and measures; his transactions were dikaiìs because they were transparent and "safe" for the customer.

  • The Mental Image: Think of a plumb line used in construction; a wall built dikaiìs is perfectly vertical. It represents the "meaning integrity" of a life that is "squared" to the truth of the Word.

4. Theological Implications Righteousness is the "sacred fitness" of the believer—s social life. Since God is "infinitely good," our dealings with others must be "unblemished" by selfishness or fraud. To live "righteously" is to show that the "internal hygiene" of the soul has resulted in an "external order." It is the refusal to let the "leprous king of self" cheat or mistreat others on the "Highway of Holiness."

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:12 The NKJV says: “...we should live soberly, righteously (dikaiìs), and godly in the present age,”. While "soberly" deals with the internal world, "righteously" deals with the external world—the believer—s relationship with their neighbor. It is the "action" of grace that ensures the believer is "reputable" in all their human interactions.

6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • 1 Corinthians 15:34: “Awake to righteousness (dikaiìs), and do not sin; for some do not have the knowledge of God.”

  • 1 Peter 2:23: “...but committed Himself to Him who judges righteously (dikaiìs);” (Referring to the "sound" judgment of God).

7. Other Relevant Insights This word represents the "transparency" of the Christian walk. To live dikaiìs is to ensure that our "spiritual container" is not leaking through acts of injustice. It is a "reproducible" testimony that grace transforms the "unhealthy" sinner into a "just" citizen, making the Gospel "becoming" to everyone who observes the believer—s life.
