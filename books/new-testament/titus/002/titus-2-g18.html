Summary

GOOD — (agathos, Strong—s G18) Refers to that which is intrinsically good, beneficial, and useful. It describes a character that is not only morally upright but also kind and generous, producing "sound" fruit that actively blesses others.

Full Study:

1. Greek Roots / Derivation A primary word. It describes goodness in the broadest sense—as a quality that is both excellent in itself and beneficial in its effect.

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition Good, pleasant, excellent, or distinguished. In the New Testament, it often refers to a "benevolent goodness"—a quality that moves beyond mere "correctness" to reach out in kindness and service.

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Fertile Soil: In secular Greek, agathos was used for soil that was "good" because it was capable of producing a rich, life-sustaining harvest.

  • The Skilled Physician: It described a doctor who was "good" not just because he knew medicine, but because his work actually restored the health of the patient.

  • The Mental Image: Think of a fruit tree that is heavy with ripe fruit; it is agathos because its internal health results in something that nourives and delights anyone who approaches it.

4. Theological Implications True goodness is an attribute of God. To be "good" in this sense is to reflect the "infinitely good" nature of the Father. It is a manifestation of the Holy Spirit, proving that a person is "wholly His." This goodness is "functional"—it works to repair what is broken and to "nourish the inner man" of those within the believer's sphere of influence.

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:5 The NKJV says: “to be discreet, chaste, homemakers, good (agathos), obedient...”. Paul includes this to ensure that the young woman's management of the home is not harsh or cold. Her "soundness" must be seasoned with a kindness that makes the "sound doctrine" attractive and "becoming" to her family.

6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • Matthew 7:17: “Even so, every good (agathos) tree bears good fruit, but a bad tree bears bad fruit.”

  • Galatians 5:22: “But the fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace, longsuffering, kindness, goodness (agathosyne—the noun form), faithfulness,”

  • Ephesians 2:10: “For we are His workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good (agathos) works...”

7. Other Relevant Insights In the "Mastering the Bible" framework, being agathos is the "overflow" of a life that has received the "goodness of God." It is the refusal to be "stagnant." A "good" woman is a "spiritual container" of grace, ensuring that her home is a place where "heaven's value" is felt through her deeds and her disposition.
