Summary

WORKS — (ergon, Strong—s G2041)

  • Summary: Refers to a deed, action, or anything accomplished by hand, art, industry, or mind.

  • Functional Truth: Works are the "fruit" of healthy doctrine; they are the tangible ways we "adorn" the gospel in our homes and workplaces .

Full Study

Greek Roots / Derivation

Derived from the primary root ergo (to work). It describes the active output or result of a specific labor or energy .

Meaning / Lexical Definition

An act, deed, or labor. In the context of Titus 2, it refers to the "pattern of good works" that Titus was commanded to model and that every believer is called to manifest.

Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Masterpiece: Used to describe the completed work of an artist or craftsman that displays their skill and character.

  • The Harvest: It carried the mental image of the gathered crop that proves the health of the seed and the soil.

  • The Mental Image: Think of a "faithful worker" whose consistent and excellent labor serves as a "living proof" that Christ transforms the heart .

Theological Implications

To be "zealous for good works" means our faith is not a static belief but a "walked out" reality . These works close the "integrity gap" by ensuring our conduct matches our confession of the "blessed hope" .

Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:14

The NKJV says: "...and purify for Himself His own special people, zealous for good (kalos) works (ergon)."

  • Paul emphasizes that these works must be "good" (kalos), meaning they are intrinsically beautiful, noble, and attractive to observers.

  • These actions are the specific means by which we ensure the "word of God" is not discredited or blasphemed by those outside the church .

Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • Matthew 5:16: "Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works (ergon) and glorify your Father..."

  • Ephesians 2:10: "For we are His workmanship, created in Christ Jesus for good works (ergon)..."

  • Titus 3:8: "...those who have believed in God should be careful to maintain good works (ergon)."

Other Relevant Insights

  • Biblical Context: While Paul often contrasts "works" with "grace" in terms of justification (Ephesians 2:8-9), in Titus 2, he shows that grace is the very power that produces these works .

  • Textual Application: These works include the daily, practical duties mentioned throughout the chapter—such as being "homemakers," "sober-minded," and "well pleasing" in the workplace .

  • Biblical Culture: In the first century, a person's "works" were their public reputation; Paul calls the church to have a reputation for excellence that "adorns the doctrine of God".
