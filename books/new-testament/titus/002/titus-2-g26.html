Summary

LOVE — (agapô, Strong—s G26)

Summary: Refers to a selfless, sacrificial, and volitional love that is rooted in the character of God rather than human emotion. It is the "bond of perfection" that demonstrates a heart is "wholly His".

Full Study:

  1. Greek Roots / Derivation: The noun form of the verb agapaì. It describes a love that is a deliberate choice of the will.

  2. Meaning / Lexical Definition: Benevolence, good will, and esteemed love. It is the divine love of God which is bestowed upon the unworthy and is demonstrated most fully in the cross.

  3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures:

    • The Unconditional Choice: In secular Greek, agapô was used to describe the "highest" form of love because it does not depend on the character of the person being loved.

    • The Royal Feast: It was later used to describe the "love feast" of the early church.

    • The Mental Image: Think of a mother—s tireless care for a child who cannot repay her; it is a love that pours out because it is its nature to give.

  4. Theological Implications: To be "sound in love" means our capacity to love is healthy and free from the "sickness" of selfishness. It is the fruit of receiving the "intoxicating love" of the Bridegroom.

  5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:2: The NKJV says: “that the older men be... sound in faith, in love (agapô)...”. Paul emphasizes this so that older men—s maturity is characterized by sacrifice rather than just cold information.

  6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

    • John 3:16: “For God so loved (agapaì) the world that He gave His only begotten Son...”

    • 1 Corinthians 13:13: “And now abide faith, hope, love (agapô), these three; but the greatest of these is love.”

    • 1 John 4:8: “He who does not love does not know God, for God is love (agapô).”

  7. Other Relevant Insights: This love is "Through His Eyes"—ascribing heaven—s value to the people around us. It is the natural "manifestation" of a life that has been "undone" by the goodness of God.
