Summary

SPECIAL — (periousios, Strong’s G4041)

In Titus 2:14, Paul defines the identity of the redeemed using a term that signifies God’s private, treasured possession. This word describes a people who are not ordinary, but are set apart for the exclusive use and pleasure of the King.

  • Refers to that which is beyond what is ordinary; a costly, private possession reserved specifically for the owner.

  • Being "special" means we have been claimed as God's personal treasure, moving us into His divine order as sons and daughters.

Full Study

Greek Roots / Derivation

Derived from peri (around/above) and ousia (being/substance), it literally means "being over and above". It describes a "peculiar" treasure that is surplus to common requirements—something specifically reserved for royal use.

Meaning / Lexical Definition

That which is one’s own; a possession belonging to oneself. In Titus 2, it refers to a people purified from "diseased" living to be "sound" and "stable" in their devotion to God.

Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The King’s Private Reserve: In ancient contexts, it referred to the specific portion of spoils that a King set aside solely for himself.

  • The Sealed Treasure: It carries the mental image of a treasure chest sealed with a signet ring, indicating the contents are for the owner's eyes only.

  • The Mental Image: Think of a "special people" who are no longer defined by the chaos of the world, but by the "Sacred Beauty" of belonging to the Bridegroom.

Theological Implications

To be special (G4041) means we have been redeemed from lawlessness to "adorn the doctrine". This identity closes the "integrity gap" because being His private possession demands a life "zealous for good works".

Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:14

The NKJV says: "...and purify for Himself His own special (periousios) people, zealous for good works."

  • Paul anchors our conduct in our ownership; because we are His "private treasure," we must live "sober-minded" and "reverent".

  • This status produces a "pattern of good works" that makes the Gospel attractive to those watching our lives.

Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • Exodus 19:5: "Then you shall be a special (periousios) treasure to Me above all people..."

  • Deuteronomy 7:6: "The Lord your God has chosen you to be a people for Himself, a special (periousios) treasure..."

  • 1 Peter 2:9: "But you are a chosen generation... His own special (periousios) people..."

Other Relevant Insights

This identity is the foundation of East Gate Culture. We live as the place where "heaven touches earth" because we are a people purified for His own possession, waiting expectantly for our Beloved.
