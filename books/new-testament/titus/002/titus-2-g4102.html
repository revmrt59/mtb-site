Summary

FAITH — (pistis, Strong’s G4102)

Summary: Refers to a settled persuasion, conviction, or trust. In the context of being "sound in faith," it describes a heart that is fully persuaded of God’s character and the truth of His Word.

Full Study:

  1. Greek Roots / Derivation: Derived from the verb peithō (to persuade or be persuaded). It is the result of God’s revelation persuading the human heart of the truth.

  2. Meaning / Lexical Definition: A firm persuasion, belief, or trust in the truth of something. It is the divinely produced conviction that God is true and His promises are certain.

  3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures:

    • The Legal Guarantee: In secular Greek, pistis was used for a legal document that served as a guarantee or a "proof" of ownership.

    • The Reliable Friend: It described the quality of a person who was completely "trustworthy" or "faithful" in their relationships.

    • The Mental Image: Think of a bridge that is solid and reliable; pistis is the confidence that the bridge will hold your weight.

  4. Theological Implications: Faith is not a "work" we perform; it is a response to the revelation of God’s goodness. To be "sound in faith" means our trust is healthy and anchored in the person of Jesus rather than our own effort.

  5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:2: The NKJV says: “that the older men be... sound (hugiainō) in faith (pistis)”. Paul is teaching that as a man ages, his trust in God should become more robust and free from the "sickness" of doubt.

  6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV):

    • Hebrews 11:1: “Now faith (pistis) is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen.”

    • Romans 10:17: “So then faith (pistis) comes by hearing, and hearing by the word of God.”

    • Ephesians 2:8: “For by grace you have been saved through faith (pistis), and that not of yourselves; it is the gift of God,”

  7. Other Relevant Insights: In the "Highway of Holiness," faith is the "embracing" phase where we receive what God provides. It is choosing to be "wholly His" through surrendered trust.
