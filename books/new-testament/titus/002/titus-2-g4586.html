Summary

REVERENT — (semnos, Strong—s G4586)

Summary: Refers to that which is honorable, dignified, and worthy of respect. It describes a man whose life carries a spiritual weight because he lives with an awareness that he is always in the presence of God.

Full Study:

  1. Greek Roots / Derivation: Derived from sebomai (to worship or revere). It carries the idea of being "venerable" or serious in a way that inspires honor.

  2. Meaning / Lexical Definition: Honorable, grave, dignified, or serious. It describes a character consistent with the "sound doctrine" being taught.

  3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures:

    • The Temple Atmosphere: Used to describe the awe-inspiring atmosphere of a sacred space.

    • The Majestic Leader: Described a leader whose conduct was so noble it commanded the respect of the city.

    • The Mental Image: Think of a person walking into a "House of Prayer"; there is a weightiness and honor in their stride because they know who they are meeting with.

  4. Theological Implications: Reverence is the outward evidence of a heart that values what God values. It is not about being "religious," but about ascribing "heaven—s value" to our time and words.

  5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:2: The NKJV says: “that the older men be sober, reverent (semnos)...”. Paul shows that as a man grows older, his life should gain a spiritual "gravitas" that makes the Gospel believable to others.

  6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV):

    • Philippians 4:8: “...whatever things are noble (semnos), whatever things are just...”

    • 1 Timothy 3:8: “Likewise deacons must be reverent (semnos), not double-tongued...”

    • 1 Timothy 3:11: “Likewise, their wives must be reverent (semnos), not slanderers...”

  7. Other Relevant Insights: Reverence is the opposite of being flippant with the things of God. It reflects choosing to "host His presence" with excellence in every area of life.
