Summary

NOT ANSWERING BACK — (antilegì, Strong—s G483) Refers to a spirit of contradiction, speaking against, or arguing. It describes a refusal to be argumentative or "talk back" in a way that is defiant, maintaining a "sound" and submissive heart that honors authority.

Full Study:

1. Greek Roots / Derivation A compound word formed from anti (against/opposite) and legì (to speak). It literally means "to speak against."

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition To contradict, gainsay, or oppose. In the context of a servant, it refers to the "unhealthy" habit of talking back, questioning orders with a defiant spirit, or engaging in "verbal rebellion" against a master.

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Obstinate Witness: In secular Greek, antilegì was used for a witness who constantly contradicted the evidence or the judge, creating "chaos" in the courtroom.

  • The Raging Stream: It pictured water hitting a rock and "pushing back" or swirling in opposition to the flow.

  • The Mental Image: Think of a "broken" seal; instead of a clean impression (typos), there is a jagged edge that refuses to take the shape of the master's will. "Not answering back" is the "internal hygiene" of a quiet and "safe" spirit.

4. Theological Implications Submission to authority is a "spiritual revelation" of our submission to God. Since God is the source of all "proper" order, to "speak against" those He has placed over us is to manifest a "diseased" heart. A life that is "wholly His" is characterized by a "sacred fitness" that trusts God—s sovereignty, even when the human master is difficult.

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:9 The NKJV says: “...to be well-pleasing in all things, not answering back (antilegì),” Paul targets this specific behavior because "talking back" was a primary way for a bondservant to show "hidden" rebellion. By refusing to engage in antilegì, the servant preserves the "meaning integrity" of their profession and keeps the "Highway of Holiness" free from the "stumbling block" of a defiant tongue.

6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • Luke 2:34: “...and for a sign which will be spoken against (antilegì),” (Referring to Christ as the sign that the world would contradict).

  • John 19:12: “...Whoever makes himself a king speaks against (antilegì) Caesar.”

  • Titus 1:9: “...that he may be able, by sound doctrine, both to exhort and convict those who contradict (antilegì).”

7. Other Relevant Insights This word represents the "shielding" of the Gospel. If a believer is known for "answering back," their words become "unhealthy" and their "spiritual container" is viewed as "contaminated." "Not answering back" ensures that the servant "adorns the doctrine" (Titus 2:10) by showing that Christ provides a "sound" mind that doesn't need to "win" every verbal battle.
