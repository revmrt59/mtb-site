Summary

TEMPERATE — (sōphrōn, Strong’s G4998)

Summary: Refers to being of a sound mind, self-controlled, and level-headed. It describes a man who has mastered his desires and impulses, keeping them in their proper place so he can remain focused on God's purposes.

Full Study:

  1. Greek Roots / Derivation: Derived from sōos (safe, sound) and phrēn (the mind). It literally means "of a safe mind" or a "sound mind".

  2. Meaning / Lexical Definition: To be of sound mind, self-controlled, or curbing one’s desires. It denotes the inner strength to remain stable under pressure.

  3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures:

    • The Skilled Charioteer: Used to describe a driver who kept firm control of his horses, ensuring the chariot stayed on the track even at high speeds.

    • The Sound Vessel: Described a ship that was seaworthy and "safe," able to withstand a storm without breaking apart.

    • The Mental Image: Think of a well-ordered house where everything is in its right place; the "temperate" man has his thoughts and emotions organized.

  4. Theological Implications: Temperance is the result of a mind renewed by the Spirit. It is about yielding our "leprous king" of self to the authority of Christ so our judgment remains clear.

  5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:2: The NKJV says: “that the older men be sober, reverent, temperate (sōphrōn)...”. Paul highlights this because older men must not be governed by the whims of the flesh; they must be examples of stability.

  6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV):

    • 1 Timothy 3:2: “A bishop then must be... sober-minded (sōphrōn), of good behavior...”

    • Titus 1:8: “...sober-minded (sōphrōn), just, holy, self-controlled,”

    • Titus 2:5: “to be discreet (sōphrōn), chaste, homemakers...”

  7. Other Relevant Insights: Being sōphrōn is a sign that the "shaking" of God has removed temporal distractions. It is a manifestation of holiness that shows a man is walking the "highway of holiness".
