PATIENCE — (hypomonô, Strong—s G5281)

Summary: Refers to a steadfast endurance or a "remaining under" pressure with a spirit that does not give up. It is a courageous perseverance that stays the course despite trials or delays.

Full Study:

  1. Greek Roots / Derivation: Derived from hypo (under) and menì (to remain/abide). It literally means "to remain under".

  2. Meaning / Lexical Definition: Steadfastness, constancy, or endurance. It is the characteristic of a believer who is not swerved from their purpose even by great trials.

  3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures:

    • The Soldier—s Fortitude: In secular Greek, hypomonô described a soldier who held his position in the heat of battle, refusing to retreat.

    • The Plant in the Storm: Used for a plant that could thrive under harsh conditions because of its internal strength.

    • The Mental Image: Think of a marathon runner who hits "the wall" but refuses to stop; they continue because their eyes are fixed on the finish line.

  4. Theological Implications: Patience is a divinely empowered capacity to endure. It is the outward proof of "soundness" in a believer—s life, showing their hope is anchored in the eternal.

  5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:2: The NKJV says: “that the older men be... sound in faith, in love, in patience (hypomonô).”. Paul emphasizes this so that older men are stable pillars that do not buckle under pressure.

  6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV):

    • Romans 5:3—4: “...knowing that tribulation produces perseverance (hypomonô); and perseverance, character; and character, hope.”

    • Hebrews 12:1: “...let us run with endurance (hypomonô) the race that is set before us,”

    • James 1:3: “...knowing that the testing of your faith produces patience (hypomonô).”

  7. Other Relevant Insights: Patience is a sign that the "shaking" of God has removed temporal distractions. It proves that holiness is a lifelong journey on the "Highway".
