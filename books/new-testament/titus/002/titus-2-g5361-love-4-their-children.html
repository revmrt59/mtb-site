Summary

LOVE THEIR CHILDREN — (philoteknos, Strong’s G5361) Refers to a mother who is devoted to and fond of her children. It describes a "sound" and nurturing affection that views the raising of children as a sacred trust and a primary expression of the Gospel within the home.

Full Study:

1. Greek Roots / Derivation A compound word formed from philos (friend, dear, or warm affection) and teknon (a child; that which is born).

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition Loving one's children; characterized by maternal affection. Like philandros, it emphasizes the "heart-connection" and natural fondness that should exist between a mother and her offspring.

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Nurturing Parent: In secular Greek literature, philoteknos was the ideal of a mother who was "wrapped up" in the well-being of her children, prioritizing their growth and protection above her own comfort.

  • The Hen and her Chicks: It carries the "picture" of a mother bird shielding and nourishing her young—a love that is both protective and tender.

  • The Mental Image: Think of a gardener who doesn't just plant a seed but delights in the daily watering and tending of the sprout; the philoteknos mother finds joy in the personhood and development of her children.

4. Theological Implications Children are a "spiritual trust" from God. To love them with philos affection is to reflect the "nourishing" heart of the Father. This word reveals that a "sound" home is one where children are not seen as a burden or a distraction, but as the primary "disciples" who are to be nurtured in the "sacred beauty" of the Lord.

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 2:4 The NKJV says: “that they admonish the young women to love their husbands, to love their children (philoteknos)...”. In the harsh environment of Crete, where life was often devalued, Paul emphasizes that "sound doctrine" must restore the natural, healthy affection of a mother. This love is a learned "discipline" that ensures the next generation is raised in an atmosphere of grace.

6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • This specific word (philoteknos) appears only here in the New Testament. It is paired with philandros to show that the young woman’s "soundness" is primarily demonstrated through her devotion to her immediate family.

7. Other Relevant Insights This term focuses on delight. It is more than just providing food and clothing; it is about "ascribing heaven's value" to the children. In the "Mastering the Bible" framework, this love is the "action" of a life that is "wholly His," ensuring that the "Highway of Holiness" begins in the nursery and the playroom.
