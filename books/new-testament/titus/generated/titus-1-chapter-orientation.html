Titus 1: Leadership that Shapes a Healthy Church

Unifying Theme

God establishes His church through qualified leaders who hold firmly to the truth and live lives that reflect the gospel within God’s design for church government.

Purpose of This Chapter:

Paul establishes that the health of the church depends on truth that leads to godliness, Biblical government and leadership appointed according to God’s design, and the willingness to confront teaching and behavior that contradict the knowledge of God. Where qualified Biblical government and leadership is lacking, disorder spreads, households are harmed, and the church is immature and unhealthy. Paul directs the church toward obedience to what God has already revealed.

Chapter Outline

  1. Paul Establishes Titus’ Authority in Crete (Titus 1:1–4)

  2. Why Titus Was Left in Crete and What Was Lacking (Titus 1:5)

  3. God’s Design for Church Leadership: Qualified Elders (Titus 1:6–9)

  4. The danger of false teachers and the need for correction (Tit 1:10–16)

Chapter Summary

Titus 1 opens by establishing Paul’s authority as a servant and apostle of Jesus Christ. Paul explains that truth is meant to produce godliness and is grounded in the hope of eternal life promised by God. Titus is left in Crete to bring order to the churches by appointing qualified elders to oversee and lead each local church. These leaders must be men of proven character who hold firmly to sound teaching. The chapter closes with a warning about false teachers whose words and lives contradict the truth and must be confronted for the health of the church.

Theological Contributions of This Chapter

• Truth is meant to lead to godly living, not just correct belief.

• Each church is to be locally governed by a plurality of qualified Elders
• Church leadership is based on character before competence.
• Sound doctrine protects God’s people from deception.
• False teaching is dangerous and must be confronted in a local body because it affects both belief and behavior.

Key Words and Concepts

Bondservant: A servant belonging to God, fully devoted to His purposes.

Truth: God’s revealed message that leads to godliness.
Elders: Qualified spiritual leaders appointed to shepherd God’s people.
Sound doctrine: Healthy teaching that aligns with the gospel.
False teachers: Those who distort the truth and disrupt the church.

Why This Chapter Matters

Titus 1 reminds us that healthy churches do not happen by accident. God cares deeply about who leads His people and how they live. The biblical model of church leadership matters because God entrusts spiritual authority to qualified Elders for the health of His people. When leadership lacks integrity or conviction, the entire church is affected. This chapter helps believers understand why government, character, truth, and courage matter in spiritual leadership.

Chapter Takeaways

• God values faithful servants who live what they teach.

• How your local church government is structured is not a secondary issue
• Leadership in the church requires tested character and sound belief.
• Truth must be guarded and defended for the good of God’s people.
• Ignoring false teaching always leads to harm.
