Summary

APOSTLE — (apostolos, Strong’s G652)
An apostle is a divinely sent representative entrusted with Christ’s authority to establish and guard revealed truth.

APOSTLE — (apostolos, Strong’s G652)

APOSTLE — (apostolos, Strong’s G652)

1. Greek roots / derivation

  • Derived from apostello, meaning “to send with authority.”

  • Denotes one commissioned as an authorized representative.

2. Meaning / definition

  • A sent one; an authorized envoy carrying the sender’s authority and message.

3. Usage in Greek culture in Biblical times (with examples if relevant)

  • In secular Greek usage, an apostolos could refer to an emissary or delegate sent with official backing.

  • In the New Testament, the term becomes specialized, referring to those personally commissioned by the risen Christ to lay the doctrinal foundation of the Church.

4. Theological implications

  • Apostolic authority is delegated, not inherent.

  • Apostles function as guardians of revealed truth, not innovators of doctrine.

5. Contextual nuance in the verse

  • Paul’s apostleship is presented as subordinate to his servanthood (“bondservant… and apostle”), reinforcing that authority flows from obedience.

6. Key uses of the word in other verses (NKJV)

  • Ephesians 4:11 And He Himself gave some to be apostles (apostolous), some prophets, some evangelists, and some pastors and teachers (NKJV)

  • 1 Corinthians 1:1 Paul (Paulos), called to be an apostle (apostolos) of Jesus Christ through the will of God (NKJV)

7. Other relevant insights (summary or comment)

  • Titus is acting under apostolic authority; his reforms in Crete are not optional suggestions.
