KNOWLEDGE — (epignìsis, StrongΓÇÖs G1922)
Refers to a precise, experiential, and participatory knowledge. It is not just "knowing about" facts, but a "full-knowledge" that takes possession of the person and results in a changed life.

Full Study:

1. Greek Roots / Derivation
Derived from epi- (upon/intensive) + gnìsis (knowledge). The prefix epi- acts as an intensifier, suggesting a knowledge that is more "focused," "complete," or "deep" than standard gnìsis. Bible Hub

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition
Precise and correct knowledge; a thorough acquaintance with the truth. In the New Testament, it almost always refers to the knowledge of God or religious truths that have a decisive influence on the soul. ThayerΓÇÖs Greek Lexicon

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Recognition: In legal or social contexts, epignìsis was used when you recognized someoneΓÇÖs face or identity after a period of time. It is the moment the "penny drops" and you fully grasp who you are looking at.

  • The Master Craftsman: While a beginner has gnìsis (basic information), a master craftsman has epignìsis—the deep, intuitive understanding of his craft that comes from years of experience.

  • The Destination: Classical writers used it to describe someone who had reached the "end point" of a search. You have moved past theories and have arrived at the actual reality.

4. Theological Implications
epignìsis is the goal of the Christian life. Paul rarely prays that people would have "power" or "stuff," but he constantly prays they would have epignìsis (Col 1:9, Eph 1:17). This implies that spiritual maturity is impossible without deep, accurate knowledge of GodΓÇÖs Word. Got Questions

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 1:1
The NKJV says: "...and the acknowledgment (epignìsis) of the truth..." Notice that faith and knowledge are linked. In Crete, people had gnìsis (they knew religious stories/myths), but they lacked epignìsis. Their "knowledge" didn't change their behavior. Paul argues that if the knowledge is truly epignìsis, it must result in godliness. BibleRef

6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • Colossians 1:9: "...filled with the knowledge (epignìsis) of His will in all wisdom..."

  • 2 Peter 1:3: "...through the knowledge (epignìsis) of Him who called us by glory and virtue."

  • 1 Timothy 2:4: "[God] desires all men to be saved and to come to the knowledge (epignìsis) of the truth."

7. Other Relevant Insights
In the NT, epignìsis is the "antidote" to false teaching. The false teachers in Titus 1:10 offered "meaningless talk," but Paul offered "full-knowledge." You cannot be deceived by a counterfeit if you have a thorough acquaintance with the genuine.
