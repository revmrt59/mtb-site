Summary

MIND — (nous, StrongΓÇÖs G3563)
Refers to the faculty of perception, understanding, and moral judgment. It is the "control center" of the human soul where information is processed, values are weighed, and decisions are made.

Full Study:

1. Greek Roots / Derivation
From the primary root noos (mind). It signifies the seat of reflective consciousness, comprising the faculties of both the intellect and the moral sentiment. Bible Hub

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition
The mind, the reason, or the intellect. In the New Testament, nous is not just the "brain" or the ability to do math; it is the organ of spiritual perception. It is the capacity to recognize GodΓÇÖs truth and apply it to life. ThayerΓÇÖs Greek Lexicon

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Pilot of the Soul: Greek philosophers (like Plato) often described the nous as the pilot or helmsman of a ship. While the "passions" (emotions) were like the wind and waves, the nous was responsible for steering the person toward virtue.

  • The Eye of the Heart: It was frequently called the "eye of the soul." Just as the physical eye allows us to see the world, the nous is the "lens" through which we "see" or understand spiritual and moral realities. Precept Austin

  • The Sieve: It functions as a filter. A healthy nous "sieves" out lies and keeps the truth; a "defiled" nous lets the truth fall through and keeps the "fables."

4. Theological Implications
The mind is the primary target of GodΓÇÖs redemptive work. Salvation involves a "renewal of the mind" (Romans 12:2). However, because of the Fall, the human nous can be "reprobate" or "darkened." When the mind is functioning correctly under the Spirit, it is the "Mind of Christ"; when it is corrupted, it becomes an engine for self-deception and sin.

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 1:15
The NKJV says: "even their mind (nous) and conscience are defiled." Paul is explaining why the false teachers are so dangerous: their "helmsman" is drunk on sin. Because their faculty of perception is "stained" (defiled), they literally cannot see the Gospel correctly. They look at the "pure" grace of God and see it as something "unclean" or "insufficient," leading them to create their own man-made rules.

6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • Romans 12:2: "...be transformed by the renewing of your mind (nous)..."

  • Philippians 4:7: "...and the peace of God... will guard your hearts and minds (nous) through Christ Jesus."

  • 1 Corinthians 2:16: "For 'who has known the mind (nous) of the Lord...?' But we have the mind (nous) of Christ."

7. Other Relevant Insights
The defilement of the nous leads to the defilement of the conscience. When the "eye" (mind) is dark, the "compass" (conscience) becomes unreadable. In Crete, the false teachers had used their intellectual "mind-power" to create "fables," proving that a brilliant nous without a holy heart is merely a tool for deception. BibleRef
