Titus 1:5 … appoint elders in every city as I commanded you-(NKJV)

1. Elders Oversee the Affairs of the Church

Elders are called to oversee and care for the local church, watching over both the direction of the church and the souls of God’s people. Their authority is exercised for the benefit of others, not personal gain.

  • Acts 20:28 - “The Holy Spirit has made you overseers…”

  • 1 Peter 5:2 - “Shepherd the flock of God which is among you…”

  • 1 Timothy 3:5 - “…how will he take care of the church of God?”

2. Elders Shepherd the Flock

Elders are shepherds, not managers. Shepherding includes leading, feeding, guiding, protecting, and caring for God’s people. Elders walk with people, not merely over them.

  • Acts 20:28 - “…to shepherd the church of God…”

  • 1 Peter 5:2–3 - “Shepherd the flock of God… being examples to the flock”

  • John 21:15–17 - “Feed My sheep”

3. Elders Govern and Direct the Church

The New Testament teaches that elders provide spiritual governance for the church. Leadership in the church rests with spiritually mature leaders called by God, not merely organizational structures or popular opinion.

  • 1 Timothy 5:17 - “The elders who rule well…”

  • Hebrews 13:7 - “Remember those who rule over you…”

  • Hebrews 13:17 - “Obey those who rule over you…”

4. Elders Disciple God’s People

Elders are responsible for helping believers grow as followers of Jesus Christ. This happens through teaching God’s Word and by living lives worthy of imitation.

  • Matthew 28:19–20 - “Make disciples…”

  • Hebrews 13:7 - “Imitate their faith”

  • 1 Corinthians 11:1 - “Imitate me, just as I also imitate Christ”

5. Elders Lead the Church

Elders lead by example, sacrifice, and service. Biblical leadership means going before others, bearing responsibility, and guiding the church according to God’s will.

  • Hebrews 13:7 - “Remember those who led you…”

  • Hebrews 13:17 - “They watch out for your souls…”

  • 1 Peter 5:3 - “Not as being lords… but being examples”

6. Elders Watch Over Souls

Elders are accountable to God for the spiritual well-being of the people entrusted to them. This requires vigilance, prayer, and deep concern for spiritual growth.

  • Hebrews 13:17 - “…for they watch out for your souls, as those who must give account”

  • Ezekiel 33:7 - Watchman principle

  • Acts 20:31 - “Therefore watch…”

7. Elders Guard and Protect the Flock

Elders must protect the church from false teaching, harmful influences, and spiritual danger. Guarding the flock is an act of love, not control.

  • Acts 20:29–31 - “Savage wolves will come in…”

  • Titus 1:9 - “Able… to exhort and convict those who contradict”

  • 2 Timothy 2:16–18 - Guarding against destructive teaching


8. Elders Care for God’s People

Elders are to care deeply and personally for people, paying attention to their needs and responding with compassion and wisdom.

  • 1 Timothy 3:5 - “Take care of the church of God”

  • Luke 10:34–35 - The Good Samaritan (same word for care)

  • 1 Thessalonians 2:7–8 - “Gentle among you… caring”

9. Elders Teach and Guard Sound Doctrine

Elders must know the truth, teach it clearly, and protect the church from error. Sound doctrine produces spiritual health.

  • Titus 1:9 - “Holding fast the faithful word…”

  • 1 Timothy 3:2 - “Able to teach”

  • 1 Timothy 5:17 - “Labor in the word and doctrine”

10. Elders Model Godly Character

Elders lead not only by instruction, but by example. Their lives must visibly reflect the gospel they proclaim.

  • 1 Peter 5:3 - “Being examples to the flock”

  • Hebrews 13:7 - “Considering the outcome of their conduct”

  • Titus 2:7 - “Show yourself a pattern of good works”

11. Elders Devote Themselves to Prayer

Elders depend on God, not human wisdom. Prayer is essential to shepherding, discernment, and spiritual authority.

  • Acts 6:4 - “We will give ourselves continually to prayer…”

  • James 5:14 - “Call for the elders… let them pray”

  • Acts 20:36 - Paul prayed with the elders

“Summary”
Biblical elders are shepherd-leaders called by God to oversee, lead, teach, protect, care for, and pray for God’s people; modeling Christlike character and guarding the health of the local church.
