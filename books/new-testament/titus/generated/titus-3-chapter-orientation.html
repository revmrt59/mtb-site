Titus 3

Unifying Theme

God’s saving mercy produces humble, obedient lives that do good works and protect the unity of the church.

Chapter Outline

  1. Living as good citizens and faithful witnesses (Tit 3:1–2)

  2. Salvation by God’s mercy, not human effort (Tit 3:3–7)

  3. Devotion to good works and rejection of divisiveness (Tit 3:8–11)

  4. Final instructions and encouragement (Tit 3:12–15)

Chapter Summary

Titus 3 turns outward to how believers live in the world and within the church. Paul reminds believers to be submissive to governing authorities, gentle toward others, and ready for good works. He grounds this call in the reality of salvation. Believers were once sinful and lost, but God saved them through His mercy, not through righteous deeds. This salvation brings new life through the Holy Spirit. Because of this, believers are to focus on good works and avoid foolish disputes and divisive behavior that harm the church’s witness.

Theological Contributions of This Chapter

• Salvation is entirely the result of God’s mercy and grace.
• Good works flow from salvation but do not earn it.
• The Holy Spirit brings renewal and new life.
• Unity in the church must be guarded against division and strife.

Key Words and Concepts

Mercy: God’s compassionate action toward those who deserve judgment.
Regeneration: The new birth in which God gives spiritual life to those who were spiritually dead.
Renewal: The ongoing inner transformation produced by the Holy Spirit.
Justification: God’s declaration that a sinner is righteous through faith, not works.
Good works: The fruit of salvation that benefits others and honors God.
Unity: Peaceful cooperation among believers that protects the church’s witness.

Why This Chapter Matters

Titus 3 helps believers understand how the gospel shapes public life and church life. Christians are called to live humbly in society while holding firmly to the truth of salvation by grace. This chapter guards against pride, legalism, and division by keeping the focus on what God has done and how His people should live in response.

High-Level Chapter Lessons

• Salvation is based on God’s mercy, not human effort.
• Good works are the fruit of salvation, not its foundation.
• The Holy Spirit renews and empowers believers for new life.
• Divisiveness damages both the church and its witness.
