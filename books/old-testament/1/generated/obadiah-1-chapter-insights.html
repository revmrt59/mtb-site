  • Obadiah challenges me to look at what makes me feel secure and where I am finding my security. Is it my bank account? My job? My gifting? My position? The chapter exposes how easily these things can take the place of trusting the Lord.

  • Pride is deceptive. It blinds us to our true state. We think we are fine and cannot even see that there is a problem. Obadiah shows how pride reassures us even as it leads us toward collapse.

  • I am responsible not only for what I do, but for how I respond when I see injustice and the suffering of others. Obadiah confronts a comfort mentality that wants to stay detached and uninvolved.

  • Taking satisfaction, even inwardly, in the misfortune of others is wicked. Obadiah exposes how gloating can exist quietly in the heart without ever being spoken out loud.

  • The flesh does not win in the end. What is driven by self-protection, pride, and advantage may appear strong for a time, but it does not last. What belongs to the Lord endures. The kingdom will be His.
