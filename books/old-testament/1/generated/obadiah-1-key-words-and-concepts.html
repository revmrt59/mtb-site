Obadiah Chapter 1: Key Words & Concepts

PRIDE ’ (zadon / gaon, Strong’s H2087 / H1347)

1. Hebrew Roots / Derivation

  • gaon (H1347) comes from a root meaning to rise, be exalted, lifted up.

  • It can refer to legitimate majesty or strength, but often carries the sense of self-exalting confidence.

  • In Obadiah 3, the emphasis is moral and spiritual, not merely emotional.

2. Meaning / Definition

Pride in this context refers to inflated self-confidence rooted in false security. It is the posture of believing one is untouchable, unaccountable, or beyond consequence.

3. Usage in Biblical Context

In the prophets, gaon often describes nations who trust in power, position, or advantage rather than the Lord (Isaiah 13:11; Jeremiah 48:29).

4. Theological Implications

Pride is not merely an internal attitude; it is a theological error. It replaces trust in God with trust in created things. Scripture consistently presents pride as the precursor to downfall.

5. Contextual Nuance in Obadiah

Edom’s pride is expressed through geography (ΓÇ£who dwell in the clefts of the rock“), security, and strategic advantage. Their pride convinces them that judgment cannot reach them.

6. Key Uses Elsewhere (NKJV)

Proverbs 16:18
Pride goes before destruction,
And a haughty spirit before a fall. (NKJV)

7. Other Insights

In Obadiah, pride is not loud arrogance. It is quiet confidence that God’s reach has limits.

DECEIVED ’ (nasha, Strong’s H5377)

1. Hebrew Roots / Derivation

nasha means to lead astray, to beguile, to deceive.
It often implies deception that is internalized and believed, not merely external trickery.

2. Meaning / Definition

To be deceived here is to accept a false assessment of reality and live accordingly.

3. Usage in Biblical Context

This verb is frequently used when people are deceived by their own assumptions or desires (Jeremiah 49:16, a close parallel to Obadiah).

4. Theological Implications

Self-deception is dangerous because it blinds a person or nation to repentance. When deception is internal, warning feels unnecessary.

5. Contextual Nuance in Obadiah

Edom is not deceived by others. Their pride deceives them. The source of deception is internal, not external.

6. Key Uses Elsewhere (NKJV)

Jeremiah 49:16
Your fierceness has deceived you,
The pride of your heart has deceived you… (NKJV)

7. Other Insights

Pride produces deception, and deception reinforces pride. Obadiah exposes this closed loop.

HEART ’ (leb / lebab, Strong’s H3820 / H3824)

1. Hebrew Roots / Derivation

The Hebrew leb refers to the inner control center of a person, not merely emotions.

2. Meaning / Definition

The heart is the seat of thought, will, intention, and trust.

3. Usage in Hebrew Thought

Unlike modern usage, the Hebrew heart thinks, plans, reasons, and decides (Proverbs 4:23).

4. Theological Implications

God addresses the heart because behavior flows from it. Judgment is rooted in what the heart trusts.

5. Contextual Nuance in Obadiah

ΓÇ£The pride of your heart“ shows that Edom’s problem is not circumstances but internal allegiance. Their heart has crowned security instead of God.

6. Key Uses Elsewhere (NKJV)

Proverbs 4:23
Keep your heart with all diligence,
For out of it spring the issues of life. (NKJV)

7. Other Insights

Obadiah shows that God does not judge only actions. He judges misplaced trust within the heart.

HOLINESS ’ (qodesh, Strong’s H6944)

1. Hebrew Roots / Derivation

qodesh means set apart, consecrated, distinct for God.
It describes what belongs to the Lord by purpose and calling.

2. Meaning / Definition

Holiness is not moral perfection alone. It is belonging to God and ordered around His presence.

3. Usage in Biblical Context

Mount Zion is repeatedly called holy because it is the place where God dwells and rules (Joel 3:17).

4. Theological Implications

Holiness is restorative. It marks a place where God’s rule is re-established after disorder.

5. Contextual Nuance in Obadiah

Holiness appears after judgment. Edom falls through pride; Zion stands through belonging. Holiness is the contrast to pride.

6. Key Uses Elsewhere (NKJV)

Joel 3:17
Then you shall know that I am the LORD your God,
Dwelling in Zion My holy mountain. (NKJV)

7. Other Insights

Obadiah shows that holiness is not isolation from judgment, but the outcome of God’s rightful rule.
