Title: Bible Study Guide to Obadiah

Description: Pastor’s Teaching manuscript

  1. Introduction

    1. Tonight we are here to study the book of Obadiah

      1. Shortest book in the Old Testament, consisting of only one chapter and 21 verses.

      2. Obadiah is considered one of "The Minor Prophets"

        1. A collection of twelve books that make up the last part of the Old Testament

        2. Augustine is credited with being the first to classify them in this way

        3. The distinction pertains only to the length of the books

          1. The "major prophets" include the books of Isaiah, Jeremiah, Lamentations, Ezekiel, Daniel

          2. The "minor prophets" are those twelve books from Hosea to Malachi

    2. Reading: Obadiah: 1:1-21

    3. When ever you go to study anything in the Bible, it is important to understand the context.

      1. Context is KING

      2. In order to understand the context and all that was going on, it is helpful to try and determine the following:

        1. Author – who wrote it? What were they going through? What was in their mind?

        2. Date – when did they write it? Helps shed light on any historical circumstances, etc.

        3. Place: Where did they write it from?

        4. Background: What was going on?

        5. Occasion: What was the purpose for writing it, in light of the background events

        6. Audience: To whom was it written?

  2. Introduction to the Book of Obadiah

    1. Author: Obadiah

      1. Obad The vision of Obadiah. Thus says the Lord GOD concerning Edom — We have heard a report from the LORD, And an envoy has been sent among the nations saying, "Arise and let us go against her for battle" — NASB

      2. His name means "Servant of the Lord” (“servant of Yahweh”)

        1. Obadiah was a very common name in the OT

        2. At least 12 people in the Bible with this name

        3. One Jewish tradition identifies him as the one who was Ahab's steward

          1. Who hid 100 prophets from Jezebel, Ahab's wife - 1 Kin 18:3-4

          2. Who feared the Lord from his youth - 1 Kin 18:12

          3. The timing does not fit

        4. We really don’t know who Obadiah was

          1. We know very little about him – nothing else in Scripture

          2. In fact, we do not even know if this was his real name - or he was just referring to himself as “the servant of the Lord”

          3. We know very little about his background, tribe, family, profession, etc.

          4. He is just “servant of the Lord”

      3. What a lesson for us right off the bat

        1. It is not about us

        2. It is not about the messenger, but about the message

        3. We are just “bondslaves”

        4. John the Baptist

          1. “Are you the Christ”

          2. 'There is One coming after me whose latches of His shoe I am not worthy to loose. I baptize you with water but He will baptize you with the Holy Ghost and with fire'. Listen to what he said, 'I must decrease and He must increase'.

          3. You see that's the voice of the prophet of God.

          4. Any prophet of God that talks about 'me, my and mine and everything that I have done and all the achievements that I have had in my life' is not the true messenger of God, because he is taken up more with the messenger than the message.

        5. The heart of a true servant of the Lord is seen in not making it about you.

    2. Date

      1. Since the Bible gives no facts about either Obadiah or his life, there is some debate about when he wrote it.

      2. Normally it is important to know when something was written, since it helps us understand context as we know what is going on and why God led him to write it.

      3. The internal clues make it clear that it was written shortly after an invasion of Jerusalem

        1. Obad 11 "On the day that you stood aloof, On the day that strangers carried off his wealth, And foreigners entered his gate And cast lots for Jerusalem — You too were as one of them. NASB

        2. We read about Jerusalem being invaded

        3. Normally that makes us immediately think of the Babylonian invasion

      4. That is one of the two dates scholars suggest for the date of writing

        1. 586 BC: Destruction of Jerusalem by the Babylonians where Jerusalem was destroyed and the people carried into captivity

        2. 845 B.C.: The days of Jehoram (848-844 B.C.), when Philistines and Arabians attacked the city
          - 2 Chr 21:8-10,16-17

        3. It is also possible it was written prophetically before the fall of Jerusalem (12-14) as the prophet sees the fall and then speaks about what Edom will do

      5. I am not going to go into the reasons for each – scholars go either way

        1. I don’t think it makes too much difference because scholars suggest two dates for when the book was written and in either case involve an attack or invasion on Jerusalem

        2. I tend to take the view that it was written at the destruction of Jerusalem

        3. My comments will reflect that

    3. Place Of Writing

      1. Nothing of known of where it was written from

      2. Obadiah could be in Jerusalem

      3. Or he could be somewhere in captivity

      4. The text seems to indicate he saw or experienced the destruction going on

      5. Sometimes events stir us to seek God and get a Word from Him

    4. Background

      1. By reading the book, we can get the picture a bit about what is taking place

      2. In one part of the picture, we have the Jews being killed and slaughtered an exiled by an invading army

        1. Jerusalem has been invaded. The walls have been broken down. The city is ablaze. Soldiers are pillaging and plundering the city.

        2. Some of the citizens are being killed

        3. Some are being taken captive (fugitives being taken into captivity

        4. Obad 14 "And do not stand at the fork of the road To cut down their fugitives; And do not imprison their survivors In the day of their distress. NASB

      3. The other part of the picture is about a people called “the Edomites” standing and watching what is happening

        1. Obad 1 The vision of Obadiah. Thus says the Lord GOD concerning Edom — … NASB

        2. Obad 11 "On the day that you stood aloof,On the day that strangers carried off his wealth, And foreigners entered his gate And cast lots for Jerusalem — You too were as one of them. NASB

        3. Obad 12 "Do not gloat over your brother's day, The day of his misfortune. And do not rejoice over the sons of Judah In the day of their destruction; Yes, do not boast In the day of their distress. NASB

        4. Obad 14 "And do not stand at the fork of the road To cut down their fugitives; And do not imprison their survivors In the day of their distress. NASB

      4. Get the picture?

        1. The Jews and Jerusalem are being destroyed by an invading army while Edom stands and watches

        2. They gloat over what is happening

        3. They are killing the fugitives and not letting them find refuge in their country.

      5. The picture gets even worse than this if we understand who the “Edomites” are?

        1. The Bible makes it clear that the Edomites were the descendents of Esau

          1. Genesis 36:8 So Esau lived in the hill country of Seir; Esau is Edom. NASB

          2. Gen 36:19 These are the sons of Esau (that is, Edom ), and these are their chiefs. NASB

        2. Meanings

          1. Esau (unknown, though some think it means ‘hairy’)

          2. Edom = red

            1. Genesis 25:25 Now the first came forth red, all over like a hairy garment; and they named him Esau. NASB

            2. Description of Esau’s physical appearance

            3. Interestingly Jacob was named according to his character

        3. The people of Edom descended from Esau, Jacob's twin brother

          1. There was sibling rivalry between Edom and Israel, found first in Esau and Jacob

            1. The twins struggled in their mother's womb - Gen 25:22-26

            2. Esau sold his birthright to Jacob - Gen 25:27-34

            3. Jacob stole Esau's blessing as the firstborn - Gen 27

          2. While Jacob and Esau eventually reconciled (Gen 32-33), their descendants were often at odds with one another

        4. Edom is also known in the Bible as Seir, Hor and Esau

        5. Territory

          1. Bordered Judah to the east and south

          2. Extended southward from the border of Moab to the Gulf of Aqaba

          3. On the eastern side Edom was bordered by desert and was ~20-30 miles wide and about ~100 miles long.

        6. Importance of Edom (James Boice)

          1. Situated along great trade routes between Syria and Egypt

            1. Trade brought business and wealth

            2. Got wealthy from tolls extracted from many caravans

          2. Edom’s natural strength and security

            1. The central area is characterized by red sandstone cliffs that rise to more than 5,000 ft above sea level.

            2. The made their homes in the cliffs

            3. Made them feel relatively secure

        7. The point is that it was their brothers who were rejoicing in their downfall and watching them be destroyed.

    6. Occasion- What was the purpose for writing it, in light of the background events

      1. To encourage the Jews about their restoration, even in the midst of calamity

      2. To warn the Edomites (brothers of the flesh) about their impending doom and judgment

  3. OUTLINE OF THE BOOK (XO)

    1. THE COMING JUDGMENT ON EDOM (1-9)

      1. The decree has gone forth to the nations (1)

      2. Deceived by pride in her location, Edom will be brought down (2-4)

      3. Destruction will be complete (5-6)

      4. Edom will be betrayed by allies (7)

      5. Not even wisdom and might can save them (8-9)

    2. THE REASON FOR JUDGMENT ON EDOM (10-16)

      1. For violence and unbrotherly conduct toward Jacob (10-11)

      2. A rebuke against such conduct (12-14)

      3. Therefore the "Day of the Lord" for them will mean receiving the same sort of treatment! (15-16)

    3. THE EXALTATION OF ISRAEL OVER EDOM (17-21)

      1. Deliverance and holiness will be found on Mt. Zion, not Mt. Seir (the prominent mountain in Edom)! (17a)

      2. The house of Jacob shall consume the house of Esau (17b-18)

      3. The children of Israel will possess Edom and surrounding nations (19-20)

      4. The ultimate rule will be that of the Lord's (21)
