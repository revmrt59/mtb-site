Summary

Pride (gaon, Strong’s H1347) is a deceptive, self-exalting confidence that shifts trust from God to false sources of security, convincing a person or nation that they are beyond accountability and therefore beyond judgment.

Full Study

PRIDE (gaon, Strong’s H1347)

1. Hebrew Roots / Derivation

Gaon (H1347) comes from a root meaning to rise, be exalted, lifted up, elevated.
At its core, the word describes height or elevation, whether physical, social, or moral.
Depending on context, it can describe legitimate majesty or sinful self-exaltation.

2. Meaning / Definition

In Obadiah 1:3, gaon refers to inflated self-confidence rooted in perceived security.
It is not mere arrogance of speech, but a settled belief that one’s position makes one safe from consequence.

3. Usage in Biblical Context

In the prophets, gaon frequently describes national or corporate pride, especially when people trust in:

  • power

  • geography

  • military strength

  • political advantage

rather than in the Lord (Isaiah 13:11; Jeremiah 48:29; Amos 6:8).

4. Theological Implications

Pride (gaon) is fundamentally a theological failure, not a personality flaw.
It elevates created advantages into functional saviors and quietly displaces trust in God.
Scripture consistently treats gaon as the condition that invites divine opposition.

5. Contextual Nuance in Obadiah 1:3

Edom’s pride is expressed through:

  • elevated geography (“clefts of the rock”)

  • strategic security

  • assumed invulnerability

Their pride tells them that judgment has limits, and that God cannot reach where they are.
This makes their pride deceptive rather than loud.

7. Other Relevant Insight

In Obadiah, pride is not boasting.
It is quiet confidence that God’s reach stops short of one’s defenses.
