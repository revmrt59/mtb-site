Summary

NATIONS — (gôy, Strong’s H1471) Refers to a people, a community, or a collective body of individuals; in a prophetic context, it specifically refers to the non-Hebrew ethnic groups or "Gentiles".

In Obadiah 1:1, the messenger is sent "among the nations." This identifies the judgment of Edom as a global "testimony". God is not only the God of Israel but the sovereign Ruler over all gôyîm, summoning them to execute His "weighty" decree against "insubordinate" pride.

Full Study

1. Word Study

  • Strong’s H1471: Meaning a nation, people, or Gentile.

  • Roots: Derived from a root meaning "to be a mass" or "a body".

  • TWOT Insight: The word originally referred to a "body" of people who were related by a common government or territory. While ‘am is often used for a "covenant people" (like Israel), gôy is frequently used for the "nations of the world" who are outside that specific covenant relationship.

2. Usage in Hebrew Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Swarming Mass: Used to describe a vast "body" of people moving together, like a "swarm of locusts" (Joel 1:6); it reflects the "chaos" that can ensue when the gôyîm are not aligned with the "Source of Truth".

  • The Territorial Boundary: It carries the image of groups defined by their "borders" rather than their "spirit," emphasizing the "natural pressure points" of geopolitical conflict.

  • The Mental Image: Think of a large field of various crops; each "nation" has its own "identity," but the Master of the Vineyard (the LORD) has authority over the harvest of every single one.

3. Theological Implications

The nations (H1471) are the theater where God displays His "integrity" and "reverence". It reflects the "divine reality" that no people group is hidden from His sight; even those who consider themselves "preeminent" or "high" are subject to His "saving health" or His judgment.

4. Contextual Nuance in Obadiah 1:1

The NKJV says: "...And a messenger has been sent among the nations (gôyîm)..."

  • Obadiah emphasizes that Edom's "evil deeds" are being broadcast to the other "nations" to serve as a "sound" warning.

  • The gôyîm are called to "rise up," proving that God can use even those who do not "know" Him to perform His "faithful" work of justice.

5. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • Genesis 12:2: "I will make you a great nation (gôy); I will bless you..."

  • Psalm 2:1: "Why do the nations (gôyîm) rage, and the people plot a vain thing?"

  • Isaiah 11:12: "He will set up a banner for the nations (gôyîm)..."

6. Insight

God’s heart is for all "nations." While we often focus on our own "natural pressure points," we must remember that the "Gospel" is intended to reach every gôy. To "walk" in the truth is to look past our own "borders" and see others as "special people" who need the "saving health" of the "Source of Truth".
