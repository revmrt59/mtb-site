Summary

KINDLE — (dālaq, Strong’s H1814) Refers to the act of setting on fire, burning, or hotly pursuing; it describes a "weighty" and intense consumption that spreads rapidly.

In Obadiah 18, the "house of Jacob" and "house of Joseph" shall "kindle" the "house of Esau". This is the "divine reality" of judgment: the "special people" who were "maliciously" pursued and "stripped" by Edom will now "hotly pursue" and consume their enemies with the "fire" of God’s "order".

Full Study

1. Word Study

  • Strong’s H1814: Meaning to burn, to kindle, or to "pursue hotly".

  • Roots: A primary root describing the ignition of a flame or the "inflamed" state of a pursuer.

  • TWOT Insight: The word captures the "natural pressure point" of a fire catching hold; it is the moment "chaos" is transformed into "destruction" for the "insubordinate".

2. Usage in Hebrew Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Hot Pursuit: Used to describe an enemy "hotly pursuing" a victim; it reflects the "violence" Edom once showed to Israel, which is now "returning upon their own head".

  • The Inflamed Eye: It carries the image of an eye "burning" with "malicious" intent or a heart "kindled" with "pride".

  • The Mental Image: Think of a spark landing in "stubble" (v. 18); to kindle (H1814) is to initiate a "complete stripping" that cannot be quenched by "empty talk".

3. Theological Implications

To kindle (H1814) is the execution of the "Day of the LORD". It proves the "integrity" of God’s Word: those who "walk" in "wickedness" provide the fuel for their own "destruction". For the "house of Esau," who "despised" their brother, the "divine reality" is a "fire" that they themselves "kindled" through their "insubordinate" actions.

4. Contextual Nuance in Obadiah 18

The NKJV says: "...They shall kindle (dālaq) them and devour them, and no survivor shall remain of the house of Esau..."

  • Obadiah uses this word to show that the judgment is active and thorough; it is not a slow burn but a "hot pursuit" that leaves "nothing".

  • This "kindling" is the "testimony" that God’s "sanctity" will eventually "consume" all "chaos".

5. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • Genesis 31:36: "...What is my sin, that you have so hotly pursued [kindled after] (dālaq) me?"

  • Psalm 7:13: "...He makes His arrows fiery [kindled] (dālaq) shafts."

  • Isaiah 5:11: "...who continue until night, till wine inflames [kindles] (dālaq) them!" (A loss of "sober" discipline).

6. Insight

To find "soul prosperity," we must ensure we are not "kindling" a "fire" of "malicious" pride in our own hearts. When we "walk" in "integrity," our lives become a "flame" of "saving health" rather than a "fire" of "destruction". Let us "reverence" the "Source of Truth," knowing that the only way to escape being "kindled" as "stubble" is to be "faithful" to the "Word of the LORD".
