Summary

SPOKEN — (peh, Strong’s H6310) While the literal Hebrew word refers to the "mouth," the NKJV translates the idiom "magnifying the mouth" as having "spoken proudly".

In Obadiah 12, God rebukes Edom, saying they should not have "spoken proudly" in the day of Israel's distress. This exposes the "integrity gap" in Edom's "walk"; they used their words to pour out "malicious" boasts and "empty talk" while their brother was experiencing "chaos" and "destruction".

Full Study

1. Word Study

  • Strong’s H6310: Meaning mouth, speech, or the opening of the face.

  • Roots: A primary root describing the physical organ of speech.

  • TWOT Insight: The term represents the "natural pressure point" where internal "pride" becomes external "violence". To "magnify the mouth" (to have spoken proudly) is to let the "insubordinate" heart find expression.

2. Usage in Hebrew Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Magnified Voice: To have "spoken proudly" carries the image of someone opening their mouth wide to "shout" over the "shame" of another.

  • The Sword's Edge: Speech is often compared to the "edge" of a sword; Edom’s "spoken" words were sharp, cutting down the "special people" with "malicious" glee.

  • The Mental Image: Think of a fountain; what is spoken reveals whether the "soul prospers" or is full of "chaos".

3. Theological Implications

What is spoken (H6310) is the primary indicator of a person’s "reverence" for God. The "Word of the LORD" warns that we will be judged by the "testimony" of our words. For Edom, having spoken proudly was the evidence of their "pride of heart," proving they were "faithful" only to their own "arrogance".

4. Contextual Nuance in Obadiah 12

The NKJV says: "...Nor should you have spoken (peh) proudly in the day of distress."

  • Obadiah notes that Edom didn't just stay silent; they actively "celebrated" the "complete stripping" of Jerusalem through what they spoken.

  • This "boasting" was a "divine reality" check: those who speak proudly against the "brethren" will find that God’s judgment speaks louder.

5. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • Exodus 4:12: "Now therefore, go, and I will be with your mouth (peh) [speech] and teach you what you shall say."

  • Psalm 19:14: "Let the words of my mouth (peh) [what is spoken]... be acceptable in Your sight..."

  • Proverbs 18:21: "Death and life are in the power of the tongue [and what is spoken]..."

6. Insight

To have "soul prosperity," we must guard what is spoken with "sober" and "temperate" discipline. When we use our words for "empty talk" and "malicious" boasting, we invite "chaos" into our own "walk". Let what is spoken be a source of "saving health" and "peace," remaining "faithful" to the "Word of the LORD".
