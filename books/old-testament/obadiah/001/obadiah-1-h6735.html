Summary

MESSENGER — (ṣîr, Strong’s H6735) Refers to an envoy, ambassador, or herald sent on a specific mission or to deliver an official message.

In Obadiah 1:1, the prophet states that a "messenger" has been sent among the nations. This signifies that the "report" from the LORD is not static; it is being actively carried out as a divine summons to the world to witness the judgment of Edom.

Full Study

1. Word Study

  • Strong’s H6735: Meaning an envoy, messenger, or ambassador.

  • Roots: Derived from a root meaning to hinge or to turn; it is related to ṣîr (H6735b), which refers to the "hinge" of a door.

  • TWOT Insight: The connection to a "hinge" suggests that the messenger is a pivotal point upon which the "spirit of the matter" turns. The messenger carries the "weight" of the sender’s authority, acting as the physical link between the "Source of Truth" and the intended recipient.

2. Usage in Hebrew Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Diplomatic Envoy: Used to describe an official representative sent to negotiate treaties or deliver declarations of war between kingdoms.

  • The Bearer of Pains: In some contexts, the same root refers to "pains" or "throes" (as in birth), suggesting that the arrival of a ṣîr can bring a "natural pressure point" or a period of intense transition.

  • The Mental Image: Think of a door swinging open; the messenger is the "hinge" that allows the "divine reality" of God’s plan to enter the room of human affairs.

3. Theological Implications

A messenger (H6735) is the instrument of God’s global "testimony". It reflects the "integrity" of a God who does not act in secret but warns the nations before bringing "chaos" or judgment. In Obadiah, the messenger’s cry—"Arise!"—serves as the "saving health" of truth for those who listen and the "destruction" of those who remain "insubordinate".

4. Contextual Nuance in Obadiah 1:1

The NKJV says: "...And a messenger (ṣîr) has been sent among the nations..."

  • The use of this term emphasizes that Edom’s downfall is not an isolated event; the "messenger" has alerted the surrounding nations to participate in God's "sovereign" decree.

  • This "envoy" from heaven carries a "spiritual weight" that overrides the "empty talk" of human diplomats, ensuring that the "Name" of the LORD is recognized across borders.

5. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • Proverbs 13:17: "A wicked messenger falls into trouble, but a faithful ambassador (ṣîr) brings health."

  • Isaiah 18:2: "...who sends ambassadors (ṣîr) by sea, even in vessels of reed on the waters..."

  • Jeremiah 49:14: "I have heard a report from the LORD, and a messenger (ṣîr) has been sent to the nations..."

6. Insight

A "messenger" reminds us that the truth must be "carried" and "delivered." We are called to be "faithful ambassadors" in our daily "walk," ensuring that the "testimony" we bear brings "peace" and "soul prosperity" rather than "chaos." To be a messenger for God is to be a "hinge" for someone else, opening the door for them to see the "divine reality" of His kingdom.
