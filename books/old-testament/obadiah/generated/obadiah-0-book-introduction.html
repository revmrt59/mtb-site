About the Book of Obadiah

Obadiah is the shortest book in the Old Testament, yet it addresses serious matters. It speaks into a moment of loss, injustice, and betrayal and reveals how the Lord responds when pride and cruelty shape human behavior.

This book is not focused on recounting history for its own sake. It shows how God views actions and responses, especially when people are suffering. Obadiah makes clear that indifference, gloating, and exploitation are not overlooked by the Lord.

The message of Obadiah presses beyond what people do to how they respond. Standing at a distance, benefiting from anotherΓÇÖs fall, or remaining silent in the face of harm carries moral weight before God.
