Summary

HOLINESS - (qodesh, Strong’s H6944)

a deceptive, self-exalting confidence that shifts trust from God to false sources of security, convincing a person or nation that they are beyond accountability and therefore beyond judgment.

Full Study

HOLINESS - (qodesh, Strong’s H6944)

1. Hebrew Roots / Derivation

qodesh means set apart, consecrated, distinct for God.
It describes what belongs to the Lord by purpose and calling.

2. Meaning / Definition

Holiness is not moral perfection alone. It is belonging to God and ordered around His presence.

3. Usage in Biblical Context

Mount Zion is repeatedly called holy because it is the place where God dwells and rules (Joel 3:17).

4. Theological Implications

Holiness is restorative. It marks a place where God’s rule is re-established after disorder.

5. Contextual Nuance in Obadiah

Holiness appears after judgment. Edom falls through pride; Zion stands through belonging. Holiness is the contrast to pride.

6. Key Uses Elsewhere (NKJV)

Joel 3:17
Then you shall know that I am the LORD your God,
Dwelling in Zion My holy mountain. (NKJV)

7. Other Insights

Obadiah shows that holiness is not isolation from judgment, but the outcome of God’s rightful rule.
