1. Greek Roots / Derivation

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition
Instruction, precept, or information. In the Pastoral Epistles, it specifically refers to the "standard of teaching" or the "apostolic deposit." It is the objective set of truths that defines the boundaries of the Christian faith. ThayerΓÇÖs Greek Lexicon

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The MasterΓÇÖs Syllabus: In Greek schools, didaskalia was the official curriculum provided by a master philosopher to his students. It wasn't a collection of random ideas, but a structured "path" of learning. Precept Austin

  • The Blueprint: A word picture associated with it is a master architect's blueprint. The "doctrine" provides the specifications for how the "house" (the church) is to be built; if the builder deviates from the blueprint, the house becomes unstable.

4. Theological Implications
Doctrine is the "backbone" of the Christian life. Without it, faith becomes mere sentiment. Because God is the Author of truth, His "doctrine" is absolute and unchangeable. It is the "good deposit" (2 Tim 1:14) that must be guarded. In Titus, doctrine is never just "head knowledge"; it is the essential foundation for "godliness" (v. 1) and "separation" to God. BibleRef
