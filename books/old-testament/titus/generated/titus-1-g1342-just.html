JUST — (dikaios, StrongΓÇÖs G1342)
Refers to a person who is upright, fair, and in conformity with GodΓÇÖs standards of righteousness. In Titus 1:8, it identifies the elder as one who is "right" in his dealings with both God and men, acting with integrity and impartiality in all his decisions. Blue Letter Bible

Full Study:

1. Greek Roots / Derivation
Derived from dikô (right, custom, or a judicial verdict). It refers to that which is "proper" or in accordance with an established standard of law or morality. Bible Hub

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition
Upright, righteous, or virtuous. It describes a person who gives to everyone their due—whether it be honor to God or fairness to a neighbor. In the New Testament, it characterizes one whose life is in harmony with the divine will. ThayerΓÇÖs Greek Lexicon

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Equal Scale: In the Greek marketplace, dikaios described a merchant whose scales were perfectly balanced. He did not cheat or show favoritism; he was "just" because he gave the exact weight promised. Precept Austin

  • The Straight Path: A word picture associated with it is a surveyorΓÇÖs line. It describes something that does not deviate to the left or the right but follows the "straight edge" of the law.

  • The Impartial Judge: It evokes the image of a judge who cannot be bribed. He does not look at the status of the person but at the "rightness" of the case.

4. Theological Implications
God is the ultimate "Just" One (Ho Dikaios). For a leader to be "just," he must reflect God's impartiality and holiness. Since the elder is a "steward" (v. 7), he must handle the MasterΓÇÖs business with total integrity. A "just" leader protects the church from the "dishonest gain" (v. 11) of the false teachers by modeling a life of transparent righteousness. BibleRef

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 1:8
The NKJV says: "...sober-minded, just (dikaios), holy..." Paul uses this to counter the "Cretan reputation." Cretans were "always liars" (v. 12); therefore, a "just" man was a living miracle in that society. An elderΓÇÖs fairness was essential for "setting in order" the church (v. 5), as he would have to make difficult decisions about discipline and doctrine without showing favoritism. Enduring Word

6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • Matthew 1:19: "Then Joseph her husband, being a just (dikaios) man..." NKJV

  • 1 John 1:9: "...He is faithful and just (dikaios) to forgive us our sins..."

  • Romans 1:17: "...as it is written, 'The just (dikaios) shall live by faith.'"

7. Other Relevant Insights
There is a distinction between "Just" and "Holy":

  • Just (Dikaios): Rightness in relation to men (fairness/integrity).

  • Holy (Hosios): Rightness in relation to God (devotion/purity).
    Together, they describe a leader who is "rightly related" in every direction—upward to Heaven and outward to his community. Precept Austin
