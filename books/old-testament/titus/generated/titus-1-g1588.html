1. Greek Roots / Derivation
Derived from ek (out of) + leg┼ì (to pick or choose). It literally means "the chosen ones" or "the picked-out ones." Bible Hub

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition
Chosen, select, or the best of its kind. It describes someone or something that has been singled out from a larger group based on the will and criteria of the one doing the choosing. In a theological sense, it refers to those whom God has chosen for Himself through Christ. ThayerΓÇÖs Greek Lexicon

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Hand-Picked Fruit: In the ancient marketplace, eklektos was used for premium goods—the "choice" produce that was set aside as the best of the harvest.

  • The Elite Troops: In military contexts, it described picked soldiers—the "special forces" or elite guards chosen for their loyalty and skill to serve the King. Precept Austin

  • The Selected Stone: It was used for choice building stones that were carefully selected by a master builder because they were fit for the foundation or the cornerstone.

4. Theological Implications
Election is a "family" term in the New Testament. It reminds the believer that they did not find God, but God found them. It is meant to produce humility and security: humility because the choice was based on God's grace, not our merit; and security because God's "choice" is not easily revoked. It shifts the focus from human performance to divine purpose.

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 1:1
The NKJV says: "...according to the faith of GodΓÇÖs elect (eklektos)..." Paul uses this term to define the scope of his mission. He isn't trying to create a new philosophy for everyone; he is a servant to the specific people God has called. By identifying the Cretan believers as "elect," Paul is giving them an incredible sense of dignity. Though the world saw them as "liars and beasts" (v. 12), God saw them as His "choice" people. 7. Other Relevant Insights
Election and "the faith" are inseparable in this verse. Paul doesn't view election as an excuse for laziness, but as a reason for evangelism and teaching. He works hard as an apostle because he knows there are "elect" people whose faith needs to be furthered and whose knowledge of the truth needs to be matured.
