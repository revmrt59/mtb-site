Summary

HOLY — (hosios, StrongΓÇÖs G3741)
Refers to a personal, inward piety and devotion that is pleasing to God. In Titus 1:8, it identifies the elder as a man who is "rightly related" to God, possessing a pure heart and a life that is set apart from the common pollutions of the world. Blue Letter Bible

Full Study:

1. Greek Roots / Derivation
A primary word (hosios) of uncertain affinity. It refers to that which is sanctioned by divine law and has a deep connection to religious duty and internal purity. Bible Hub

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition
Undefiled by sin, free from wickedness, and religiously pure. While hagios (another word for holy) often refers to being "set apart" for a task, hosios focuses on the inward quality of a personΓÇÖs relationship with God—their "heart-piety" and personal devotion. ThayerΓÇÖs Greek Lexicon

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Unpolluted Stream: In secular Greek, hosios was used for water that was ceremonially clean and fit for use in a temple. It describes a life that is free from the "mud" and "sediment" of worldly corruption. Precept Austin

  • The Upright Altar: A word picture associated with it is a private altar in a home. It represents the "secret life" of prayer and devotion that happens when no one else is watching.

  • The Untainted Hand: It evokes the image of "lifting up holy hands" (1 Tim 2:8)—hands that are clean because they have not been involved in "dishonest gain" or "evil" deeds.

4. Theological Implications
God is the "Holy One" (Ho Hosios). For a leader to be hosios, he must have a conscience that is clear before God. This is the spiritual engine of his ministry. Since the elder must "refute" (v. 9) those who are "defiled" (v. 15), his own life must be a "holy" contrast. Holiness is the internal shield that protects the "Steward of God" from the "beastly" culture around him. BibleRef

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 1:8
The NKJV says: "...just, holy (hosios), self-controlled..." Paul uses this to address the "Cretan virus." The Cretans were "defiled" in their minds and consciences (v. 15). By requiring the elder to be hosios, Paul is demanding a man whose inner life is healthy. If he is not "holy," his "profession" (v. 16) is just empty talk. Enduring Word

6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • 1 Timothy 2:8: "...lifting up holy (hosios) hands, without wrath and doubting." NKJV

  • Acts 13:35: "...You will not allow Your Holy (hosios) One to see corruption." (Referring to Christ).

  • Hebrews 7:26: "For such a High Priest was fitting for us, who is holy (hosios), harmless, undefiled..."

7. Other Relevant Insights
There is a profound distinction between hosios and dikaios (Just):

  • Just (Dikaios): Righteousness in his horizontal duties toward men.

  • Holy (Hosios): Righteousness in his vertical duties toward God.
    A man who is "just" but not "holy" is merely a moralist; a man who is "holy" but not "just" is a hypocrite. The elder must be both. Precept Austin
