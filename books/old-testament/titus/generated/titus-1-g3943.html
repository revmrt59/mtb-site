Summary

GIVEN TO WINE — (paroinos, StrongΓÇÖs G3943)
Refers to a person who is habitually under the influence of alcohol or characterized by brawling, quarrelsome behavior resulting from drink. In Titus 1:7, it describes a man whose judgment and self-control are compromised by wine, making him a "shameful" representative of the Gospel. Blue Letter Bible

Full Study:

1. Greek Roots / Derivation
A compound word from para (beside) + oinos (wine). It literally means "one who sits long beside his wine" or "one who stays near the wine-cup." Bible Hub

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition
Addicted to wine; drunken, or abusive under the influence. It identifies more than just an occasional drink; it points to a fixed habit or a lifestyle where alcohol is a constant companion, leading to "tipsy" arrogance and verbal or physical aggression. ThayerΓÇÖs Greek Lexicon

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Tavern Brawler: In secular Greek, paroinos was often used for the violent drunkard who picks fights. It evokes the image of a "brawl at a banquet" where wine has removed the "governor" of civilized behavior. Precept Austin

  • The "Furniture" of the Pub: A word picture associated with it is a man who is as much a part of the tavern as the tables and benches. He is always "beside the wine," never far enough away to be sober and vigilant.

  • The Compromised Watchman: It describes a sentry who drinks on duty. Because his senses are dulled, he cannot "oversee" (episkopos) anything effectively.

4. Theological Implications
Leadership requires a "sober mind" (s┼ìphron) to discern the will of God and the needs of the flock. Alcohol is a "mocker" (Proverbs 20:1) that displaces the Holy SpiritΓÇÖs control. A leader "beside wine" cannot be "beside the Word." Since an elder is a "steward of God" (v. 7), he must not be under the power of any substance that would lead him to "deny Him by his works" (v. 16). BibleRef

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 1:7
The NKJV says: "...not quick-tempered, not given to wine (paroinos)..." Paul is contrasting the elder with the "lazy gluttons" of Crete (v. 12). In a culture famous for excess, an elderΓÇÖs temperance was a revolutionary credential. If he was paroinos, he would naturally be "quick-tempered" and "violent," destroying the very order he was appointed to set (v. 5). Enduring Word

6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)

  • 1 Timothy 3:3: "...not given to wine (paroinos), not violent, not greedy for money..." NKJV

  • Note: This specific adjective appears only twice in the NT (1 Tim 3:3 and Titus 1:7), specifically for church leaders.

  • Ephesians 5:18: "And do not be drunk with wine (oinos)... but be filled with the Spirit."

  • Proverbs 23:29-30 (LXX): "Who has woe?... Those who stay long at the wine (oinos)..."

7. Other Relevant Insights
The paroinos man is the opposite of the "Self-Controlled" man (enkrat─ôs) in verse 8. One is led by the "cup," the other by the "Spirit." Paul demands a leader who is "drunk on the Truth," not one whose spiritual "vision" is blurred by fermented grapes. Precept Austin
