3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures

  • The Overturned Tables: This is the same word used in John 2:15 when Jesus "overturned the tables" of the moneychangers in the temple. The mental image is of a complete scattering of everything that was once orderly and in its place.

  • The Capsize: In secular Greek writings, it was frequently used for a ship being capsized or a building being demolished. It suggests a disaster that leaves the original structure unusable.

  • The Household Upset: The word indicates a total disruption. If one member of a family is perverted by false teaching, the "whole house" is upset, like a table being flipped over, causing everything on it to fall and break.

4. Theological Implications
Subversion is the direct opposite of "edification" (building up). It reveals that false doctrine is not a victimless crime; it is an active force of demolition. When the "unruly" teach for dishonest gain, they are not just sharing opinions—they are dismantling the very foundations of the faith in those they influence.
