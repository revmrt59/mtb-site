ABLE ΓÇö (dynatos, StrongΓÇÖs G1415)
Refers to being possessed of power, strength, or ability. In Titus 1:9, it identifies the elder as a man who is spiritually "competent" and "empowered" by the Word to perform the active duties of leadership, specifically in the defense and promotion of the Gospel. Blue Letter Bible┬á

Full Study:┬á

1. Greek Roots / Derivation
Derived from dynamai (to be able/to have power). It is the root of the English word "dynamic" or "dynamite." It signifies having the necessary "force" to achieve a result. Bible Hub┬á

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition
Powerful, mighty, or capable. It describes one who has the capacity, whether through natural talent or divine endowment, to excel in a particular task. In the New Testament, it often refers to being "mighty in word and deed." ThayerΓÇÖs Greek Lexicon┬á

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures┬á

  • The Competent Soldier: In secular Greek, dynatos described a soldier who was fit for battle. He didn't just have the rank; he had the "muscle" and the skill to actually defeat the enemy.

  • The Weight-Lifter: A word picture associated with it is a man who can lift a heavy load. While others buckle under the weight of "lacking" things (v. 5) or false teachers (v. 10), the dynatos leader has the strength to carry the burden. Precept Austin

  • The Full Battery: It evokes the image of a source of power that is "charged up." The elder is "able" because he is connected to the "power-grid" of the pistos Word.┬á

4. Theological Implications
God is the "Mighty One" (Ho Dynatos). Human ability is always a derivative of God's power. Paul is clear that our "sufficiency is from God" (2 Cor 3:5). An elder is "able" not because of his IQ or personality, but because he is clothed with the power of the Word. It is a "functional holiness"ΓÇöhis separation to God (hagios) results in him being "empowered" by God (dynatos) for the work of the ministry. BibleRef┬á

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 1:9
The NKJV says: "...that he may be able (dynatos), by sound doctrine, both to exhort and convict..." This is the "so what?" of the qualifications. A man can be "blameless" and "hospitable," but if he is not able to handle the Word, he cannot be an elder. In Crete, the elders had to be "spiritual heavyweights" to knock down the "meaningless talk" (v. 10) of the deceivers. Enduring Word┬á

6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)┬á

  • Romans 4:21: "...fully convinced that what He had promised He was also able (dynatos) to do." NKJV

  • Luke 24:19: "...Jesus of Nazareth, who was a Prophet mighty (dynatos) in deed and word before God..."

  • Acts 18:24: "...Apollos... an eloquent man and mighty (dynatos) in the Scriptures, came to Ephesus."┬á

7. Other Relevant Insights
There is a specific sequence here: Holding Fast (tenacity) leads to being Able (competency). You cannot be "able" to defend the truth if you are not "holding fast" to it. The elder's "ability" is surgically precise; he uses "sound doctrine" to "exhort" (build up) and "convict" (shut down). Precept Austin┬á
