1. Greek Roots / Derivation
A primary word (katharos) signifying "clean" or "clear." It is the root of the English word "catharsis" (a cleansing or purging). Bible Hub┬á

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition
Free from impure admixture; without blemish or spot. Metaphorically, it refers to a soul that is "clean" from the guilt and power of sin. It describes a state of "singleness" where the heart is not divided by hidden agendas or hypocrisy. ThayerΓÇÖs Greek Lexicon┬á

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures┬á

  • The Refined Gold: In Greek metallurgy, katharos described pure gold that had been passed through the fire to remove dross. It was "100% gold" with no cheaper metals mixed in.

  • The Clear Water: It was used for pristine spring water that was free from mud or sediment. It was "clean" enough to drink and "clear" enough to see through to the bottom.

  • The Pruned Vine: In agriculture, it described a pruned branch that had been "cleaned" of dead wood so it could produce more fruit. Precept Austin

  • The Discharged Soldier: In legal contexts, it was used for someone whose debts were cleared or who had been "purged" of any legal charges.┬á

4. Theological Implications
Purity in the New Testament is primarily an internal reality rather than an external ritual. While the "circumcision group" (v. 10) focused on "pure foods" and "pure rituals," Paul argues that true purity is a result of the Gospel "purifying the heart by faith" (Acts 15:9). A "pure" person is someone whose motives and mind have been aligned with GodΓÇÖs truth.┬á

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 1:15
The NKJV says: "To the pure (katharos) all things are pure." This is a direct strike against the "commandments of men" (v. 14). Paul is teaching that for the believer whose conscience is clean through Christ, external things like food or Jewish rituals cannot "defile" them. The purity of the heart acts as a filter that sees and uses GodΓÇÖs creation for His glory. BibleRef┬á

6. Key Uses in Other Verses (NKJV)┬á

  • Matthew 5:8: "Blessed are the pure (katharos) in heart, for they shall see God."

  • 1 Timothy 1:5: "Now the purpose of the commandment is love from a pure (katharos) heart..."

  • James 1:27: "Pure (katharos) and undefiled religion before God and the Father is this..."┬á

7. Other Relevant Insights
There is a profound contrast between the "pure" and the "defiled." If the "lens" of the heart is katharos, everything it looks at is seen through the light of GodΓÇÖs goodness. However, if the "lens" is dirty (defiled), even holy things are twisted into something sinful. Purity is the spiritual health that allows the believer to enjoy God's world without being enslaved by it.┬á
