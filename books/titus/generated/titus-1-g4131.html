Refers to a person who is prone to physical or verbal aggression. In Titus 1:7, it describes a "bully" who uses force or "sharp blows" of the tongue to get their way, making them unfit to shepherd a flock that requires gentle care and persuasive truth. Blue Letter Bible┬á

1. Greek Roots / Derivation
Derived from pl─ôss┼ì, which means "to strike" or "to smite." It describes one who is ready with a blow. Bible Hub┬á

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition
A bruiser, a striker, or a pugnacious person. It refers to someone who settles disputes with their fists or through "striking" verbal abuse. It is the opposite of being "gentle" or "patient." ThayerΓÇÖs Greek Lexicon┬á

3. Usage in Greek Culture & Word Pictures┬á

  • The Street Fighter: In secular Greek, pl─ôkt─ôs described a combative man who was quick to start a physical altercation. It evokes the image of a "brawler" who uses his strength to intimidate others. Precept Austin

  • The Lashing Tongue: A word picture associated with it is a whip or a lash. Just as a whip leaves a physical welt, a pl─ôkt─ôs leader leaves emotional and spiritual welts on people through harsh, "hitting" words.

  • The Out-of-Control Hammer: It describes a tool that is smashing everything in its path rather than building anything up. Instead of "setting in order" (v. 5), the striker "breaks down."┬á

4. Theological Implications
The "servant of the Lord must not quarrel but be gentle to all" (2 Timothy 2:24). A pl─ôkt─ôs leader fails to reflect the character of Christ, who "when He was reviled, did not revile in return" (1 Peter 2:23). Since an elder is a "steward of God" (v. 7), he must lead by spiritual influence, not by "striking" fear or using physical/emotional force. BibleRef┬á

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 1:7
The NKJV says: "...not given to wine, not violent (pl─ôkt─ôs)..." (Note: Some NKJV editions translate this as "not a striker"). Paul links this directly to being "given to wine" because alcohol often releases this violent impulse. In the "beastly" culture of Crete (v. 12), violence was a sign of "strength." Paul redefines strength as self-restraint. A violent man can never "set in order" a church because he creates chaos wherever he goes. Enduring Word┬á

7. Other Relevant Insights

A "striker" may win an argument through force, but he loses the soul of the person he is trying to lead. Precept Austin┬á
