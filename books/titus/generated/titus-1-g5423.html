1. Greek Roots / Derivation

2. Meaning / Lexical Definition
A mind-misleader; a seducer of the understanding. It describes someone who manipulates the "judgment center" of another person so that they can no longer distinguish between sound doctrine and destructive myths.┬á

4. Theological Implications
The mind is the primary battlefield of the Christian life (Romans 12:2). Paul identifies these deceivers as "mind-misleaders" because if the enemy can corrupt the process of thinking, he doesn't have to keep lying about individual factsΓÇöthe person will eventually begin generating their own errors.┬á

5. Contextual Nuance in Titus 1:10
Paul is warning Titus that these individuals are not just "uninformed." They are predatory.
