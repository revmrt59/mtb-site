Title: Why Study the Old Testament

Description: Nine Reasons Why We Should Study the Old Testament

Nine Reasons Why We Should Study the Old Testament?

  1. All Scripture is Profitable NOTES

    2. 2 Timothy 3:16 All Scripture is inspired by God and profitable for teaching, for reproof, for correction, for training in righteousness; NAS

    3. Therefore the OT is profitable for:

      1. Doctrine - such as the nature of God, man, and sin

      2. Reproof and correction - the need for repentance

      3. Instruction in righteousness - how to live godly lives

        1. Hebrews 11:4 By faith Abel offered to God a better sacrifice than Cain… NAS

        2. Hebrews 11:33 … who by faith conquered kingdoms, performed acts of righteousness, … NAS

    4. The NT writers frequently appealed to the OT in instructing Christians concerning their conduct:

      1. James 2:21 Was not Abraham our father justified by works, when he offered up Isaac his son on the altar? NAS

      2. James 2:25 And in the same way was not Rahab the harlot also justified by works, when she received the messengers and sent them out by another way? NAS

      3. James 5:17-18 Elijah was a man with a nature like ours, and he prayed earnestly that it might not rain; and it did not rain on the earth for three years and six months. 18 And he prayed again, and the sky poured rain, and the earth produced its fruit. NAS

      4. James 5:11 Behold, we count those blessed who endured. You have heard of the endurance of Job and have seen the outcome of the Lord's dealings, that the Lord is full of compassion and is merciful. NAS

  2. Necessary to Properly Understand Scripture

    1. 2Ti 2:15 Study to shew thyself approved unto God, a workman that needeth not to be ashamed, rightly dividing the word of truth. (KJV)

      1. Here we are told to explicitly study Scripture if we are going to understand it

    2. Four reasons why it is necessary to study the OT to properly understand Scripture

      1. Necessary for Proper Hermeneutics

        1. Some of the basic hermeneutic principles requiring the study of the OT

          1. First mentioned principle

          2. Complete mentioned principle

          3. Progressive mentioned principle

            1. Isaiah 28:13 So the word of the LORD to them will be, "Order on order, order on order, Line on line, line on line, A little here, a little there," …NAS

            2. Progressive revelation means that God did not give His full truths all at once, rather He unfolded them, step by step, detail by detail, each portion giving further amplification and clarification.

          4. Typological principle - figures and shadows

            1. Hebrews 8:5 who serve a copy and shadow of the heavenly things, just as Moses was warned by God when he was about to erect the tabernacle; for, "See," He says, "that you make all things according to the pattern which was shown you on the mountain." NAS

            2. Colossians 2:16-17 Therefore let no one act as your judge in regard to food or drink or in respect to a festival or a new moon or a Sabbath day-- 17 things which are a mere shadow of what is to come; but the substance belongs to Christ. NAS

            3. Hebrews 9:24 For Christ did not enter a holy place made with hands, a mere copy of the true one, but into heaven itself, now to appear in the presence of God for us; NAS

      2. Necessary to Understand NT Texts

        1. Jude 11 Woe to them! For they have gone the way of Cain, and for pay they have rushed headlong into the error of Balaam, and perished in the rebellion of Korah. NAS

        2. John 3:14 "And as Moses lifted up the serpent in the wilderness, even so must the Son of Man be lifted up; NAS

        3. Divorce

        4. Matthew 4:4 But He answered and said, "It is written, 'Man shall not live on bread alone, but on every word that proceeds out of the mouth of God.'" NAS

        5. Ps 22:1 My God, my God, why hast thou forsaken me? why art thou so far from helping me, and from the words of my roaring? (KJV)

      3. Necessary to Understand Biblical Prophecy

        1. Dr. Charles Hodge: ΓÇ£The subject of prophecy cannot be adequately discussed without taking a survey of all the prophetic teachings in Scripture both of the OT and the NT.

      4. Necessary to Understand the Book of Revelation

        1. Revelation alone has at least 620 OT allusions and references.

        2. Seven spirits of Christ in Revelation

          1. Rev 1:4 John to the seven churches which are in Asia: Grace be unto you, and peace, from him which is, and which was, and which is to come; and from the seven Spirits which are before his throne; (KJV)

          2. Isa 11:1-2 And there shall come forth a rod out of the stem of Jesse, and a Branch shall grow out of his roots: 2 And the spirit of the LORD shall rest upon him, the spirit of wisdom and understanding, the spirit of counsel and might, the spirit of knowledge and of the fear of the LORD; (KJV)

        3. Sea

          1. Revelation 4:6 and before the throne there was, as it were, a sea of glass like crystal; and in the center and around the throne, four living creatures full of eyes in front and behind. NAS

          2. Isaiah 57:20 But the wicked are like the tossing sea, For it cannot be quiet, And its waters toss up refuse and mud. NAS

  3. The OT was Written for Our Warning and Benefit

    1. 1 Cor 10:11 Now all these things happened unto them for ensamples: and they are written for our admonition, upon whom the ends of the world are come. KJV

    2. The NT writers frequently appealed to the OT in admonishing and warning Christians

      1. 1Cor 10

      2. Jesus: Luke 17:32 "Remember Lot's wife. NAS

      3. Hebrews 3:12-19

      4. As James, in encouraging Christians to be patient in their suffering - Ja 5:7-11

      5. As Peter, in warning of false teachers and scoffers

        1. 2 Peter 2:6 and if He condemned the cities of Sodom and Gomorrah to destruction by reducing them to ashes, having made them an example to those who would live ungodly thereafter; NAS

        2. 2 Peter 2:15 forsaking the right way they have gone astray, having followed the way of Balaam, the son of Beor, who loved the wages of unrighteousness, NAS

      6. Jude 11 Woe to them! For they have gone the way of Cain, and for pay they have rushed headlong into the error of Balaam, and perished in the rebellion of Korah. NAS

  4. The OT was Written to Teach Us

    1. Romans 15:4 For whatever was written in earlier times was written for our instruction, that through perseverance and the encouragement of the Scriptures we might have hope. NAS

    2. The NT writers invested the historical events of the OT with prophetic significance.

      1. The crossing of the Red Sea under Israel prefigured Christian baptism and all that it implies (1Cor 10:1-2).

      2. Joshua’s conquest of Canaan typified the spiritual rest into which NT believes enter by faith (Heb 3:4).

        1. Hebrews 4:8 For if Joshua had given them rest, He would not have spoken of another day after that. NAS

    3. The OT helps us understand the dealings, principle and character of God


  5. The OT was Written to Give us Hope

    1. Romans 15:4 For whatever was written in earlier times was written for our instruction, that through perseverance and the encouragement of the Scriptures we might have hope. NAS

    2. The OT provides a record of God's faithfulness, how He kept His promises.

  6. As a Source of Encouragement

    1. Ephesians 5:19 speaking to one another in psalms and hymns and spiritual songs, singing and making melody with your heart to the Lord; NAS

  7. To Makes One Wise Regarding Salvation in Christ

    1. 2 Timothy 3:14-15 You, however, continue in the things you have learned and become convinced of, knowing from whom you have learned them; 15 and that from childhood you have known the sacred writings which are able to give you the wisdom that leads to salvation through faith which is in Christ Jesus. NAS

    2. One cannot hope to fully understand such books of the New Testament like:

      1. Hebrews, without an understanding of the Levitical priesthood [Exodus, Leviticus]

      2. Revelation, without an understanding of OT prophecy and apocalyptic literature

    3. Remember The Whole Bible is About Christ and the Redemption of Man

      1. Hebrews 10:7 "Then I said, 'Behold, I have come (In the roll of the book it is written of Me) To do Thy will, O God.'" NAS

  8. Necessary to Understand God’s Plan for Israel

    1. God has a special place for Israel in His plan. The OT helps reveal that plan

    2. Romans 9:13 Just as it is written, "Jacob I loved, but Esau I hated." NAS

  9. Produce a Sense of Awe/Worship

    1. History really is HIS-STORY - Story of God ruling in the affairs of men

    2. The Bible is not two testaments or books, but really one book in organic unity focused on one single theme — the revelation of a great God and His redemption of fallen man.
